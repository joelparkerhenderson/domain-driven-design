# Subdomains: Attention-Deficit Domain

Subdomains classify the areas of the ADHD management domain by their strategic importance, complexity, and differentiation potential. This classification guides investment decisions, determining where to allocate the most domain expertise, where to adopt standardized solutions, and where to leverage existing frameworks.

## Purpose

Not all parts of the ADHD management domain are equally complex or strategically important. Subdomain classification ensures that the most valuable and differentiating aspects of the system receive the deepest modeling effort, while more generic aspects leverage established patterns and standards. This prevents over-engineering commodity functions and under-investing in areas that provide the greatest clinical and organizational value.

## Core Subdomains

Core subdomains represent the most strategically important and differentiating areas of the ADHD management domain. They require deep domain expertise, custom modeling, and continuous refinement.

### Clinical Assessment Subdomain

Diagnostic evaluation is the foundation of all ADHD management. The complexity of multi-informant assessment, differential diagnosis, comorbidity identification, and DSM-5 criteria application requires sophisticated domain modeling. This subdomain differentiates high-quality ADHD care from routine screening. It encompasses rating scale selection and interpretation, neuropsychological test battery configuration, clinical interview protocols, and diagnostic formulation logic.

### Treatment Management Subdomain

Multimodal treatment planning is where clinical judgment, evidence-based practice, and individual patient characteristics intersect. The complexity of coordinating behavioral therapy, parent training, coaching, psychoeducation, and environmental modifications into a coherent treatment plan requires deep domain expertise. Treatment sequencing, modality selection based on presentation type and severity, and adaptive plan modification based on treatment response all demand custom modeling.

## Supporting Subdomains

Supporting subdomains enable core processes and add significant value but follow more standardized patterns. They require domain knowledge but can leverage established frameworks.

### Behavioral Monitoring Subdomain

Symptom tracking and behavior charting follow well-established behavioral science methodologies. While the data collected is essential for treatment decisions and outcomes evaluation, the monitoring patterns themselves (frequency recording, duration measurement, interval sampling, ABC charting) are standardized. The modeling challenge lies in adapting these established frameworks to the specific symptom dimensions and functional domains relevant to ADHD.

### Educational Accommodation Subdomain

IEP and 504 plan management follows regulatory frameworks established by IDEA and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act. While the accommodation planning process is critical for students with ADHD, the structural elements (eligibility determination, accommodation selection, plan review cycles) are governed by legal requirements. The modeling challenge centers on translating clinical assessment findings into educationally relevant recommendations.

## Generic Subdomains

Generic subdomains solve necessary but common problems that do not differentiate the ADHD management system. They can leverage existing frameworks, standards, and off-the-shelf solutions.

### Medication Management Subdomain

Pharmacological treatment follows established clinical pharmacology principles. Medication selection, dosage titration, side effect monitoring, and adherence tracking are well-documented processes governed by prescribing guidelines and drug reference databases. While medication management is clinically important, the domain logic follows standard pharmaceutical patterns that do not require custom modeling beyond ADHD-specific titration protocols.

### Outcomes Tracking Subdomain

Treatment effectiveness measurement uses established psychometric and statistical methods. Standardized outcome measures, pre-post comparison designs, and quality of life instruments are generic measurement frameworks applied across many clinical domains. The ADHD-specific aspects are primarily in the selection of relevant outcome measures and the interpretation of results within ADHD clinical context.

## Strategic Investment Implications

| Subdomain | Type | Investment Level | Modeling Approach |
|---|---|---|---|
| Clinical Assessment | Core | Highest | Deep custom modeling with domain experts |
| Treatment Management | Core | High | Custom modeling with evidence-based frameworks |
| Behavioral Monitoring | Supporting | Moderate | Adapted standard behavioral science frameworks |
| Educational Accommodation | Supporting | Moderate | Regulatory-driven framework adaptation |
| Medication Management | Generic | Lower | Standard pharmacological frameworks |
| Outcomes Tracking | Generic | Lower | Standard measurement and analytics frameworks |
