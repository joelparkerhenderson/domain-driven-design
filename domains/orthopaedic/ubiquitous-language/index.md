# Orthopaedic Domain - Ubiquitous Language

Arthroplasty: A surgical procedure to restore joint function by resurfacing or replacing a damaged joint with a prosthetic implant.

Arthroscopy: A minimally invasive surgical technique using a small camera and instruments inserted through small incisions to diagnose and treat joint conditions.

AO/OTA Classification: The standardized alphanumeric system maintained by the AO Foundation and Orthopaedic Trauma Association for categorizing fractures by bone, segment, and morphology.

Bearing Surface: The articulating interface between implant components in a joint replacement, such as metal-on-polyethylene or ceramic-on-ceramic.

Body Mass Index (BMI): A calculated value from height and weight used as a risk factor in surgical planning and outcome prediction.

Clinical Assessment: The systematic process of evaluating a patient's musculoskeletal condition through history, physical examination, and diagnostic imaging.

Closed Reduction: A non-surgical technique to realign fractured bone fragments by external manipulation without incision.

Comminuted Fracture: A fracture in which the bone is broken into three or more fragments, requiring complex fixation strategies.

Debridement: The surgical removal of damaged, dead, or infected tissue to promote healing and prevent further complications.

Fixation: The stabilization of fractured bone using internal devices (plates, screws, nails) or external frames to maintain alignment during healing.

Gait Analysis: The systematic measurement and evaluation of walking patterns to assess lower extremity function and guide rehabilitation.

Goniometry: The measurement of joint range of motion using a goniometer instrument, recorded in degrees.

Implant Registry: A systematic database that tracks implant data, patient outcomes, and revision rates for surveillance and quality improvement.

Joint Range of Motion: The measurable arc of movement available at a joint, expressed in degrees from a defined neutral position.

Malunion: The healing of a fracture in a non-anatomical position, potentially requiring corrective osteotomy.

Meniscus: The C-shaped fibrocartilage structure in the knee that provides shock absorption, load distribution, and joint stability.

Nonunion: The failure of a fracture to heal within the expected time frame, typically defined as no healing progress after six months.

Open Reduction Internal Fixation (ORIF): A surgical procedure involving incision to expose the fracture site, anatomical reduction, and stabilization with internal hardware.

Orthopaedic Outcome Score: A validated patient-reported measure quantifying functional status and quality of life, such as the Oxford Hip Score or WOMAC.

Osteotomy: A surgical procedure in which bone is cut to shorten, lengthen, or realign it to correct deformity or redistribute mechanical load.

Patient-Reported Outcome Measure (PROM): A standardized questionnaire completed by patients to assess their perception of health status and treatment effectiveness.

Periprosthetic Fracture: A fracture occurring around or adjacent to an existing orthopaedic implant, requiring specialized management.

Range of Motion Protocol: A structured rehabilitation program defining the permitted and targeted joint movement at each phase of recovery.

Reduction: The restoration of displaced bone fragments or a dislocated joint to their normal anatomical alignment.

Return-to-Play Protocol: A graduated, criteria-based progression that an athlete must complete before resuming competitive sports after injury.

Revision Surgery: A subsequent surgical procedure performed to replace, modify, or remove a previously implanted orthopaedic device.

Rotator Cuff: The group of four muscles and tendons (supraspinatus, infraspinatus, teres minor, subscapularis) that stabilize and move the shoulder joint.

Surgical Templating: The pre-operative process of overlaying implant templates on radiographic images to determine optimal component size and positioning.

Tendon Repair: The surgical reattachment or reconstruction of a torn tendon to restore musculoskeletal function.

Weight-Bearing Status: The prescribed amount of load a patient may place on an injured or operated extremity during recovery, ranging from non-weight-bearing to full weight-bearing.
