# Ergonomics - Ubiquitous Language

- **Anthropometry**: The scientific measurement of human body dimensions, proportions, and physical capabilities, used to design workstations, equipment, and tools that accommodate the target user population.

- **Awkward Posture**: A body position that deviates significantly from neutral alignment, increasing biomechanical stress on muscles, tendons, and joints and elevating the risk of musculoskeletal injury.

- **Contact Stress**: Localized pressure on soft tissues caused by sustained contact with hard surfaces or sharp edges, such as resting wrists on a desk edge, which can compress nerves and blood vessels.

- **Cumulative Trauma Disorder (CTD)**: An injury to the musculoskeletal or nervous system resulting from the accumulated effect of repeated physical stresses over time, also known as a repetitive strain injury.

- **Duty Cycle**: The ratio of work time to rest time within a task, used to evaluate exposure duration and determine whether work-rest schedules are adequate to prevent fatigue and injury.

- **Engineering Control**: An ergonomic intervention that modifies the physical design of equipment, tools, or the work environment to reduce or eliminate exposure to risk factors at the source.

- **Force**: The physical effort exerted by a worker to perform a task, such as gripping, pushing, pulling, or lifting, measured as a risk factor because excessive or sustained force contributes to musculoskeletal disorders.

- **Job Rotation**: An administrative control strategy in which workers alternate between tasks with different physical demands to distribute biomechanical loading across different muscle groups and reduce cumulative exposure.

- **Manual Handling**: Any activity requiring a worker to lift, lower, push, pull, carry, or otherwise move a load by hand or bodily force, subject to risk assessment under ergonomics regulations.

- **Musculoskeletal Disorder (MSD)**: An injury or condition affecting the muscles, nerves, tendons, ligaments, joints, cartilage, or spinal discs, commonly caused or aggravated by workplace ergonomic risk factors.

- **Neutral Posture**: The natural, balanced alignment of the body in which joints are not bent or twisted and muscles are at their resting length, minimizing biomechanical stress and injury risk.

- **OWAS (Ovako Working Posture Analysing System)**: A standardized observational method for classifying and evaluating working postures based on the positions of the back, arms, and legs and the load handled.

- **Percentile**: A statistical value indicating the proportion of a population whose body dimension falls at or below a given measurement, commonly the 5th, 50th, and 95th percentiles used in workstation design.

- **REBA (Rapid Entire Body Assessment)**: A standardized postural assessment tool that evaluates the ergonomic risk of whole-body tasks by scoring trunk, neck, leg, upper arm, lower arm, and wrist positions along with force and coupling factors.

- **Repetition**: The frequency with which a specific motion or exertion is performed within a given time period, identified as a primary ergonomic risk factor when combined with force or awkward posture.

- **Risk Factor**: A workplace condition or exposure, such as excessive force, awkward posture, repetition, vibration, or contact stress, that increases the probability of developing a musculoskeletal disorder.

- **RULA (Rapid Upper Limb Assessment)**: A standardized assessment tool that evaluates the ergonomic risk of upper limb disorders by scoring posture, muscle use, and force for the arms, wrists, neck, and trunk.

- **Strain Index**: A semi-quantitative job analysis method that estimates the risk of distal upper extremity musculoskeletal disorders by combining ratings of intensity of exertion, duration of exertion, efforts per minute, hand/wrist posture, speed of work, and duration of task per day.

- **Vibration Exposure**: The transmission of mechanical oscillation to the human body through hand-arm vibration (from powered tools) or whole-body vibration (from vehicles or platforms), which can cause vascular, neurological, and musculoskeletal damage.

- **Work-Rest Schedule**: A planned pattern of work periods alternating with recovery breaks, designed to allow physiological recovery and prevent the accumulation of fatigue that leads to musculoskeletal injury.

- **Workstation Assessment**: A systematic evaluation of a worker's physical work environment, including furniture dimensions, equipment placement, posture, and task demands, conducted to identify ergonomic risk factors and recommend improvements.
