# Heart Health Metrics - Ubiquitous Language

Heart Rate: The number of times the heart beats per minute, measured in beats per minute (bpm), serving as a fundamental vital sign for cardiac health assessment.

Resting Heart Rate: The heart rate measured when a patient is calm and stationary, typically between 60 and 100 bpm for healthy adults, used as a baseline cardiac fitness indicator.

Heart Rate Variability (HRV): The variation in time intervals between consecutive heartbeats (R-R intervals), reflecting autonomic nervous system function and cardiac adaptability.

Blood Pressure: The force exerted by circulating blood on arterial walls, expressed as systolic over diastolic pressure in millimeters of mercury (mmHg).

Systolic Pressure: The peak arterial pressure during ventricular contraction (systole), representing the maximum force of blood ejection from the heart.

Diastolic Pressure: The minimum arterial pressure during ventricular relaxation (diastole), representing the resting pressure between heartbeats.

Hypertension: Persistently elevated blood pressure exceeding clinical thresholds (typically 130/80 mmHg per ACC/AHA guidelines), increasing cardiovascular disease risk.

Electrocardiogram (ECG/EKG): A recording of the heart's electrical activity over time using surface electrodes, producing waveforms that reveal rhythm, conduction, and structural information.

QRS Complex: The ECG waveform representing ventricular depolarization, whose morphology and duration indicate conduction pathway integrity and ventricular function.

P Wave: The ECG waveform representing atrial depolarization, whose presence, shape, and timing indicate atrial rhythm and atrioventricular conduction status.

T Wave: The ECG waveform representing ventricular repolarization, whose morphology can indicate ischemia, electrolyte imbalance, or other cardiac pathology.

R-R Interval: The time between consecutive R peaks in the ECG, used to calculate heart rate and heart rate variability metrics.

QT Interval: The time from the start of the QRS complex to the end of the T wave, representing total ventricular electrical activity duration, with prolongation indicating arrhythmia risk.

Arrhythmia: An abnormal heart rhythm caused by irregular electrical impulse generation or conduction, ranging from benign to life-threatening.

Atrial Fibrillation (AFib): An irregular and often rapid heart rhythm originating from chaotic atrial electrical activity, significantly increasing stroke risk.

Bradycardia: A heart rate below 60 bpm that may indicate athletic conditioning, medication effects, or pathological conduction system disease.

Tachycardia: A heart rate exceeding 100 bpm at rest, which may indicate stress, dehydration, fever, or underlying cardiac pathology.

Cardiac Output: The volume of blood the heart pumps per minute, calculated as stroke volume multiplied by heart rate, reflecting overall cardiac performance.

Ejection Fraction: The percentage of blood pumped out of the left ventricle with each contraction, serving as a primary measure of heart pump function.

Framingham Risk Score: A sex-specific algorithm used to estimate the 10-year cardiovascular risk of an individual based on age, cholesterol, blood pressure, diabetes status, and smoking history.

ASCVD Risk Score: The Atherosclerotic Cardiovascular Disease risk calculator recommended by ACC/AHA, estimating 10-year and lifetime risk using demographic and clinical factors.

Cardiovascular Risk Factor: A measurable characteristic or exposure that increases the probability of developing cardiovascular disease, such as hypertension, diabetes, or hyperlipidemia.

Cardiac Biomarker: A measurable substance in blood that indicates cardiac damage, stress, or dysfunction, such as troponin, BNP, or CRP.

Troponin: A protein released into the bloodstream when heart muscle is damaged, serving as the primary biomarker for diagnosing acute myocardial infarction.

BNP (B-type Natriuretic Peptide): A hormone released by ventricular myocytes in response to wall stress, used as a biomarker for heart failure diagnosis and management.

Photoplethysmography (PPG): An optical technique used in wearable devices to detect blood volume changes in microvascular tissue, enabling non-invasive heart rate and SpO2 measurement.

FHIR (Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources): An HL7 standard for exchanging healthcare information electronically, providing RESTful APIs and standardized resource definitions for clinical data.

LOINC (Logical Observation Identifiers Names and Codes): A universal coding system for identifying medical laboratory observations and clinical measurements, ensuring interoperable health data exchange.

SNOMED CT: A comprehensive clinical terminology system providing standardized codes for diseases, symptoms, procedures, and findings used in electronic health records.

Clinical Decision Support (CDS): A system capability that provides clinicians with patient-specific assessments and recommendations derived from analyzed cardiac health metrics and evidence-based guidelines.
