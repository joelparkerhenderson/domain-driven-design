# Legal Sector Domain - Ubiquitous Language

- **Attorney-Client Privilege**: A legal doctrine that protects confidential communications between an attorney and their client made for the purpose of seeking or providing legal advice from compelled disclosure.

- **Billable Hour**: A unit of time, typically recorded in six-minute (0.1 hour) increments, that represents attorney or paralegal work directly attributable to a client matter and eligible for invoicing.

- **Case Disposition**: The final outcome or resolution of a legal matter, such as dismissal, settlement, judgment, or withdrawal, that formally closes the case in the court or firm's records.

- **Conflict of Interest**: A situation in which an attorney's duties to a current or prospective client are materially adverse to the interests of another current or former client, potentially disqualifying the attorney or firm from representation.

- **Contingency Fee**: A fee arrangement in which the attorney's compensation is a predetermined percentage of the recovery obtained for the client, with no fee owed if the case is unsuccessful.

- **Continuing Legal Education (CLE)**: Mandatory professional development courses required by state bar associations for licensed attorneys to maintain their active bar membership and stay current on legal developments.

- **Docket**: A formal record maintained by a court listing all filings, motions, orders, and proceedings in a particular case, or an internal firm calendar tracking deadlines across all matters.

- **E-Discovery**: The process of identifying, collecting, preserving, reviewing, and producing electronically stored information (ESI) in response to discovery requests in litigation or regulatory proceedings.

- **Engagement Letter**: A written agreement between an attorney or law firm and a client that defines the scope of representation, fee arrangement, billing practices, and respective responsibilities.

- **Escrow**: A financial arrangement in which funds or assets are held by a neutral third party on behalf of the transacting parties, released upon the fulfillment of specified contractual conditions.

- **Flat Fee**: A predetermined fixed amount charged for a defined legal service regardless of the actual time spent, commonly used for routine matters such as incorporations, wills, or uncontested proceedings.

- **IOLTA (Interest on Lawyers' Trust Accounts)**: A program requiring attorneys to deposit client funds that are nominal in amount or held for a short duration into a pooled interest-bearing trust account, with the earned interest directed to fund legal aid services.

- **Litigation Hold**: A directive issued to preserve all documents and electronically stored information potentially relevant to pending or reasonably anticipated litigation, suspending routine document destruction.

- **Matter**: A discrete legal engagement or case that a law firm handles on behalf of a client, serving as the primary organizational unit for tracking work, time, expenses, and documents.

- **Motion**: A formal written request submitted to a court asking the judge to issue a ruling or order on a specific legal issue before, during, or after trial.

- **Paralegal**: A qualified professional who assists attorneys by performing substantive legal work under attorney supervision, including legal research, document preparation, and case management tasks.

- **Privilege Log**: A document listing materials withheld from discovery production on the basis of attorney-client privilege or work product doctrine, providing sufficient detail for the opposing party to assess the privilege claim.

- **Retainer**: An advance payment made by a client to a law firm to secure legal services, typically deposited into a trust account and drawn down as fees are earned or costs incurred.

- **Statute of Limitations**: A law prescribing the maximum time period within which legal proceedings must be initiated after the occurrence of the event giving rise to the claim, after which the right to sue is barred.

- **Subpoena**: A court-issued order compelling a person to appear for testimony (subpoena ad testificandum) or to produce documents and tangible evidence (subpoena duces tecum) in a legal proceeding.

- **Trust Account**: A separate bank account maintained by a law firm to hold client funds distinct from the firm's operating funds, subject to strict ethical rules governing deposits, withdrawals, and reconciliation.
