# Otolaryngology Domain - Ubiquitous Language

## Clinical Assessment Terms

- **Audiogram**: A graphical representation of hearing thresholds at specific frequencies, used to diagnose the type and degree of hearing loss.
- **Endoscopy**: A minimally invasive diagnostic procedure using a flexible or rigid scope to visualize the nasal cavity, pharynx, and larynx.
- **Tympanometry**: An objective test of middle ear function that measures tympanic membrane compliance in response to pressure changes.
- **Head and Neck Examination**: A systematic physical assessment of the ears, nose, throat, oral cavity, neck, and related structures.
- **Imaging Study**: Diagnostic imaging such as CT, MRI, or ultrasound used to evaluate sinonasal, temporal bone, or head/neck pathology.

## Surgical Management Terms

- **Tonsillectomy**: Surgical removal of the palatine tonsils, commonly performed for recurrent tonsillitis or obstructive sleep apnea.
- **Septoplasty**: Surgical correction of a deviated nasal septum to improve nasal airflow and reduce obstruction.
- **Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery (FESS)**: Minimally invasive surgery using endoscopes to treat chronic sinusitis by restoring sinus drainage pathways.
- **Thyroidectomy**: Surgical removal of all or part of the thyroid gland for nodules, cancer, or hyperthyroidism.
- **Surgical Case**: An aggregate representing a planned or completed ENT surgical procedure, including pre-operative evaluation, intra-operative details, and post-operative follow-up.

## Voice Disorders Terms

- **Laryngoscopy**: Examination of the larynx and vocal folds using a mirror, flexible scope, or rigid telescope.
- **Voice Handicap Index (VHI)**: A patient-reported outcome measure quantifying the perceived impact of a voice disorder on daily life.
- **Vocal Fold Pathology**: Any structural or functional abnormality of the vocal folds, including nodules, polyps, cysts, and paralysis.
- **Phonosurgery**: Surgical procedures performed on the larynx to improve or restore voice function.
- **Voice Therapy Protocol**: A structured program of behavioral exercises and techniques to rehabilitate disordered voice production.

## Sleep Disorders Terms

- **Apnea-Hypopnea Index (AHI)**: The number of apnea and hypopnea events per hour of sleep, used to classify obstructive sleep apnea severity.
- **Polysomnography (PSG)**: An overnight sleep study recording physiological parameters to diagnose sleep-disordered breathing.
- **CPAP Therapy**: Continuous positive airway pressure delivered via a mask to maintain upper airway patency during sleep.
- **Uvulopalatopharyngoplasty (UPPP)**: Surgical removal of excess tissue in the throat to widen the airway and reduce obstructive sleep apnea.
- **Epworth Sleepiness Scale (ESS)**: A validated questionnaire measuring daytime sleepiness as an indicator of sleep disorder severity.

## Allergy Management Terms

- **Skin Prick Test**: An in-vivo allergy test where allergen extracts are introduced into the epidermis to assess immediate hypersensitivity reactions.
- **Immunotherapy Protocol**: A structured treatment plan involving escalating doses of allergen extracts to induce immune tolerance.
- **Allergic Rhinitis**: Inflammation of the nasal mucosa caused by IgE-mediated hypersensitivity to environmental allergens.
- **Chronic Rhinosinusitis (CRS)**: Persistent inflammation of the nasal and paranasal sinus mucosa lasting twelve or more weeks.
- **Allergen Panel**: A defined set of allergen extracts used in diagnostic testing, categorized by type (aeroallergen, food, venom).

## Hearing Services Terms

- **Hearing Evaluation**: A comprehensive audiological assessment including pure tone audiometry, speech audiometry, and immittance testing.
- **Hearing Aid Fitting**: The process of selecting, programming, and verifying a hearing aid device matched to a patient's audiometric profile.
- **Cochlear Implant Evaluation**: A multidisciplinary assessment of candidacy for cochlear implantation based on audiological, medical, and psychosocial criteria.
- **Otoacoustic Emissions (OAE)**: Sounds generated by outer hair cells of the cochlea, measured to assess cochlear function.
- **Aural Rehabilitation**: A program of auditory training, communication strategies, and counseling to maximize benefit from hearing devices.

## Cross-Cutting Terms

- **ENT Encounter**: A clinical visit or interaction between an otolaryngology provider and a patient, serving as the primary unit of clinical documentation.
- **Clinical Pathway**: A standardized, evidence-based care plan that defines the expected sequence of assessments, interventions, and outcomes for a specific ENT condition.
