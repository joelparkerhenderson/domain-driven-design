# Hearing Services Context - Otolaryngology Domain

## Overview

The Hearing Services Context provides comprehensive audiological care within the otolaryngology domain. It covers the full spectrum of hearing healthcare, from diagnostic hearing evaluation and hearing loss classification through hearing aid selection, fitting, and verification, cochlear implant candidacy evaluation, and aural rehabilitation. This context captures the specialized workflows of audiologists who manage patients with hearing and balance disorders in collaboration with otolaryngologists.

## Subdomain Classification

Hearing Services is a supporting subdomain. Audiological evaluation and hearing aid fitting follow standardized protocols published by the American Academy of Audiology and the American Speech-Language-Hearing Association. Cochlear implant candidacy criteria follow FDA-approved guidelines. While custom modeling is needed to capture the longitudinal nature of hearing care and the relationship between audiometric data and device selection, the underlying clinical protocols are well-established.

## Ubiquitous Language

- **Audiological Record**: The primary aggregate representing a patient's complete hearing history, device history, and rehabilitation status.
- **Hearing Evaluation**: A comprehensive audiological assessment including pure tone audiometry, speech audiometry, and immittance testing.
- **Audiogram**: A graphical record of hearing thresholds at specific frequencies (250 Hz to 8000 Hz) for air and bone conduction.
- **Hearing Aid Fitting**: The process of selecting, programming, and verifying a hearing aid matched to a patient's audiometric profile and communication needs.
- **Cochlear Implant Evaluation**: A multidisciplinary assessment of candidacy for cochlear implantation based on audiological, medical, and psychosocial criteria.
- **Aural Rehabilitation**: A program of auditory training, communication strategies, and counseling to maximize benefit from hearing devices.
- **Otoacoustic Emissions (OAE)**: Sounds generated by cochlear outer hair cells, measured to assess cochlear function.

## Aggregate: Audiological Record

The Audiological Record is the primary aggregate root, maintaining a longitudinal record of a patient's hearing status and hearing healthcare over time.

### Entities Within the Aggregate

- **Hearing Evaluation**: Records a comprehensive audiological assessment including audiometric thresholds, speech recognition scores, tympanometric results, and OAE results.
- **Hearing Aid Fitting**: Tracks device selection, programming parameters, real-ear verification measurements, and patient-reported outcome measures.
- **Cochlear Implant Evaluation**: Documents candidacy assessment including aided audiometric testing, speech perception testing in best-aided condition, CT/MRI imaging review, and multidisciplinary team recommendation.
- **Aural Rehabilitation Plan**: Tracks rehabilitation goals, auditory training sessions, communication strategy counseling, and functional outcome measures.

### Key Value Objects

- **Audiometric Threshold**: Frequency, hearing level (dB HL), ear, conduction type (air, bone), masking status.
- **Speech Recognition Score**: Word list, presentation level, score (percentage correct), ear.
- **Tympanometric Result**: Peak compliance, peak pressure, ear canal volume, tympanogram type (A, As, Ad, B, C).
- **Hearing Loss Classification**: Type (conductive, sensorineural, mixed), degree (normal through profound), configuration (flat, sloping, rising, notched).
- **OAE Result**: Emission type (TEOAE, DPOAE), frequency-specific results, overall pass/refer determination.
- **Real-Ear Measurement**: Target gain, measured gain, residual (deviation from target) at each frequency.
- **Hearing Aid Specification**: Manufacturer, model, style, technology level, feature set, ear (left, right, bilateral).

### Invariants

- A hearing aid fitting requires a current audiogram (within six months).
- Hearing aid verification must include real-ear measurements demonstrating adequate fit to prescriptive targets.
- Cochlear implant candidacy evaluation requires documented hearing aid trial with appropriately fitted devices.
- Speech perception testing for cochlear implant candidacy must use validated recorded materials in best-aided condition.
- Audiometric testing must follow calibration standards (ANSI S3.6) and use appropriate masking when interaural attenuation thresholds are exceeded.
- An audiological record must track laterality consistently (left, right, bilateral) across all evaluations and fittings.

## Domain Events Published

- **HearingEvaluationCompleted**: Publishes evaluation results including hearing loss classification and recommendations.
- **HearingAidFitted**: Publishes fitting details including device, verification results, and patient satisfaction.
- **CochlearImplantCandidacyDetermined**: Publishes candidacy determination and supporting criteria.
- **AuralRehabilitationMilestoneReached**: Publishes rehabilitation progress and achievement of functional goals.

## Domain Events Consumed

- **AudiometricScreeningCompleted** (from Clinical Assessment): Receives screening results that trigger comprehensive audiological evaluation.
- **SurgeryCompleted** (from Surgical Management): Receives cochlear implant surgery completion to initiate device activation planning and post-surgical audiological care.

## Domain Services

- **HearingLossClassificationService**: Classifies hearing loss type, degree, and configuration from audiometric data. Assesses interaural asymmetry against AAO-HNS guidelines for retrocochlear evaluation.
- **CochlearImplantCandidacyService**: Evaluates cochlear implant candidacy based on audiological criteria (severe-to-profound SNHL, limited aided speech perception), medical clearance, and psychosocial readiness.

## Clinical Workflow

1. Patient referred for hearing evaluation (from Clinical Assessment or direct referral).
2. Comprehensive audiological assessment performed (pure tone audiometry, speech audiometry, immittance, OAE).
3. Hearing loss classified by type, degree, and configuration.
4. Recommendations determined (monitoring, hearing aids, cochlear implant evaluation, medical referral).
5. If hearing aids: Device selection based on audiometric profile, lifestyle, and preferences.
6. Hearing aids programmed and fitted with real-ear verification.
7. Follow-up adjustments and patient-reported outcome measurement.
8. If cochlear implant: Candidacy evaluation with aided speech perception testing, imaging, and multidisciplinary review.
9. If CI candidate: Referral to Surgical Management for implantation, followed by device activation and aural rehabilitation.

## Integration Points

- **Clinical Assessment Context**: Receives audiometric screening results; provides comprehensive evaluation reports for ENT documentation.
- **Surgical Management Context**: Partnership for cochlear implant and ear surgery cases, with bidirectional information flow.
- **Voice Disorders Context**: Shared kernel for communication assessment in patients with combined voice and hearing disorders.
