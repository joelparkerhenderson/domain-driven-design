# Cardiology Domain - Ubiquitous Language

Ejection Fraction (EF): The percentage of blood pumped out of the left ventricle with each heartbeat, a primary measure of cardiac systolic function.

Left Ventricular Ejection Fraction (LVEF): The specific ejection fraction measurement for the left ventricle, used to classify heart failure subtypes and guide therapy.

Heart Failure with Reduced Ejection Fraction (HFrEF): Heart failure characterized by LVEF of 40% or less, indicating impaired systolic function.

Heart Failure with Preserved Ejection Fraction (HFpEF): Heart failure characterized by LVEF of 50% or greater, indicating diastolic dysfunction with maintained systolic function.

Guideline-Directed Medical Therapy (GDMT): Evidence-based pharmacological treatment regimens recommended by ACC/AHA guidelines for specific cardiac conditions.

Percutaneous Coronary Intervention (PCI): A minimally invasive procedure using catheter-based techniques to open blocked coronary arteries, typically involving balloon angioplasty and stent placement.

Transcatheter Aortic Valve Replacement (TAVR): A catheter-based procedure to replace a diseased aortic valve without open-heart surgery.

Cardiac Catheterization: An invasive diagnostic procedure involving the insertion of a catheter into the heart chambers or coronary arteries to assess anatomy and function.

Echocardiography: An ultrasound-based diagnostic imaging modality used to visualize cardiac structure, function, and hemodynamics in real time.

Cardiac Biomarker: A measurable substance in blood whose presence or concentration indicates cardiac injury, stress, or disease, such as troponin or BNP.

Troponin: A cardiac-specific protein released into the bloodstream during myocardial injury, serving as the primary biomarker for acute myocardial infarction diagnosis.

B-type Natriuretic Peptide (BNP): A neurohormone released by ventricular cardiomyocytes in response to volume overload and wall stress, used to diagnose and monitor heart failure severity.

Stress Testing: A diagnostic evaluation that assesses cardiac function under physiological or pharmacological stress to detect ischemia, arrhythmias, or exercise intolerance.

Risk Stratification: The systematic process of categorizing patients into risk groups based on clinical, laboratory, and imaging data to guide diagnostic and therapeutic decisions.

Arrhythmia: An abnormal heart rhythm resulting from disordered electrical impulse generation or conduction within the cardiac conduction system.

Atrial Fibrillation (AFib): The most common sustained cardiac arrhythmia, characterized by rapid and irregular atrial electrical activity replacing normal sinus rhythm.

Ablation: A therapeutic procedure that uses radiofrequency energy, cryotherapy, or pulsed field energy to destroy abnormal cardiac tissue responsible for arrhythmias.

Implantable Cardioverter-Defibrillator (ICD): A surgically implanted device that continuously monitors heart rhythm and delivers electrical shocks to terminate life-threatening ventricular arrhythmias.

Pacemaker: An implantable electronic device that delivers electrical impulses to regulate heart rate when the native conduction system fails to maintain adequate rhythm.

Electrophysiology Study (EP Study): An invasive diagnostic procedure that records intracardiac electrical activity to map arrhythmia mechanisms and guide ablation therapy.

Coronary Artery Disease (CAD): A pathological condition characterized by atherosclerotic plaque accumulation in the coronary arteries, leading to reduced myocardial blood supply.

Myocardial Infarction (MI): Irreversible death of myocardial tissue resulting from prolonged ischemia, commonly known as a heart attack.

Cardiac Rehabilitation: A medically supervised program combining exercise training, risk factor modification, education, and psychosocial support for patients recovering from cardiac events or procedures.

Cardiac Output: The volume of blood pumped by the heart per minute, calculated as the product of stroke volume and heart rate.

Hemodynamics: The study of blood flow dynamics within the cardiovascular system, including pressures, volumes, and resistances across cardiac chambers and vessels.

Valvular Heart Disease: A condition involving dysfunction of one or more heart valves, causing stenosis (narrowing) or regurgitation (leaking) that impairs cardiac efficiency.

Mechanical Circulatory Support (MCS): Device-based therapies such as ventricular assist devices (VADs) and intra-aortic balloon pumps that augment or replace native cardiac pump function.

SYNTAX Score: An angiographic scoring system that quantifies the complexity of coronary artery disease to guide revascularization strategy decisions between PCI and CABG.

CHA2DS2-VASc Score: A clinical prediction rule for estimating stroke risk in patients with atrial fibrillation, used to guide anticoagulation therapy decisions.

NYHA Functional Classification: A four-class system developed by the New York Heart Association to categorize heart failure severity based on patient symptom burden during physical activity.

ACC/AHA Stage Classification: A four-stage (A through D) classification system for heart failure progression that captures the full continuum from risk factors to advanced refractory disease.
