# Dentistry - Ubiquitous Language

- **Bitewing Radiograph**: An intraoral dental radiograph that captures the crowns and interproximal areas of the upper and lower teeth on a single film, used primarily to detect interproximal caries and assess alveolar bone levels.

- **Caries**: The disease process resulting in the localized destruction of tooth hard tissues (enamel, dentin, cementum) by acid produced by bacterial fermentation of dietary carbohydrates, commonly known as tooth decay or cavities.

- **Crown**: A fixed prosthetic restoration that covers the entire visible portion of a tooth above the gumline, used to restore structural integrity, function, and aesthetics to a severely damaged or weakened tooth.

- **Dental Chart**: A standardized graphical and textual record of all teeth present, their condition, existing restorations, missing teeth, and pathology, using either the FDI two-digit notation or the Universal Numbering System.

- **Dental Prophylaxis**: A professional cleaning procedure performed by a dental hygienist or dentist to remove plaque, calculus, and stain from tooth surfaces above and below the gingival margin.

- **Endodontic Treatment**: A procedure to treat infection or inflammation within the tooth pulp, most commonly root canal therapy, involving the removal of pulp tissue, shaping and disinfection of root canals, and obturation with a filling material.

- **Extraction**: The surgical or non-surgical removal of a tooth from its socket in the alveolar bone, indicated when a tooth is non-restorable, severely infected, or for orthodontic or prosthetic reasons.

- **FDI Notation**: The two-digit tooth numbering system adopted by the Federation Dentaire Internationale in which the first digit indicates the quadrant and the second digit identifies the tooth within that quadrant.

- **Fluoride Application**: A preventive procedure in which a concentrated fluoride agent is applied topically to tooth surfaces to strengthen enamel, promote remineralization, and inhibit demineralization.

- **Gingival Recession**: The apical migration of the gingival margin exposing the root surface of a tooth, which can result from periodontal disease, aggressive brushing, or anatomical factors.

- **Implant**: A titanium or zirconia fixture surgically placed into the jawbone to serve as an artificial tooth root, onto which a prosthetic crown, bridge, or denture is attached after osseointegration.

- **Occlusion**: The relationship between the upper (maxillary) and lower (mandibular) teeth when they come into contact during biting, chewing, or jaw closure, classified as normal, malocclusion, or traumatic.

- **Periapical Radiograph**: An intraoral dental radiograph that shows the entire tooth from crown to root apex and the surrounding bone, used to diagnose periapical pathology, root morphology, and alveolar bone loss.

- **Periodontal Probing**: A clinical procedure in which a calibrated probe is inserted into the gingival sulcus at multiple sites around each tooth to measure pocket depth and assess periodontal health.

- **Pit and Fissure Sealant**: A resin or glass-ionomer material applied to the occlusal surfaces of posterior teeth to fill anatomical grooves and prevent caries in susceptible pits and fissures.

- **Probing Depth**: The measurement in millimeters from the gingival margin to the base of the periodontal pocket, recorded at six sites per tooth to assess periodontal disease severity.

- **Recall Interval**: The recommended time between routine dental examinations and preventive visits, determined by individual patient risk factors including caries rate, periodontal status, and oral hygiene effectiveness.

- **Restoration**: A procedure or the resulting material placed to repair tooth structure lost to caries, fracture, or wear, including direct fillings (composite, amalgam) and indirect restorations (inlays, onlays, crowns).

- **Root Canal**: The anatomical space within the root of a tooth that contains the dental pulp, or the treatment procedure (root canal therapy) that removes infected pulp and seals the canal system.

- **Scaling and Root Planing**: A non-surgical periodontal therapy involving the removal of calculus and bacterial biofilm from tooth surfaces and root surfaces below the gingival margin, followed by smoothing of root surfaces.

- **Surface Designation**: The standardized naming of the five surfaces of a tooth (mesial, distal, buccal/labial, lingual/palatal, occlusal/incisal) used to describe the location of caries, restorations, and other findings.

- **Temporomandibular Joint (TMJ)**: The bilateral synovial joint connecting the mandible to the temporal bone of the skull, dysfunction of which can cause pain, clicking, locking, and limited jaw movement.

- **Treatment Plan**: A documented sequence of proposed dental procedures prioritized by clinical urgency and patient preference, including estimated costs, number of visits, and required specialist referrals.
