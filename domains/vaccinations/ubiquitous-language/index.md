# Vaccinations Domain - Ubiquitous Language

- **Adverse Event Following Immunization (AEFI)**: Any untoward medical occurrence that follows immunization and does not necessarily have a causal relationship with the vaccine, ranging from local injection site reactions to systemic events requiring medical intervention.

- **Booster Dose**: An additional dose of a vaccine administered after the primary series to restore waning immunity and extend protection against the target disease.

- **Catch-Up Schedule**: A modified immunization timeline calculated for patients who have missed one or more recommended vaccine doses, specifying the minimum intervals between doses to bring the patient up to date as quickly and safely as possible.

- **Cold Chain**: The temperature-controlled system of storage and transport that maintains vaccines within their required temperature range (typically 2 to 8 degrees Celsius) from manufacture through administration to preserve potency.

- **Combination Vaccine**: A single vaccine product that contains antigens for multiple diseases in one injection, such as MMR (measles, mumps, rubella) or DTaP (diphtheria, tetanus, pertussis), reducing the number of injections needed.

- **Contraindication**: A condition in the recipient that significantly increases the risk of a serious adverse reaction to a specific vaccine, making administration of that vaccine medically inadvisable.

- **CVX Code**: A standardized numerical code assigned by the CDC to identify specific vaccine products, used in immunization information systems and electronic health records for interoperable dose tracking.

- **Dose**: A single measured quantity of a vaccine product administered to a patient, recorded with the vaccine type, lot number, site, route, date, and administering provider.

- **Exemption**: A legal provision allowing a patient to forgo one or more required vaccinations based on medical, religious, or philosophical grounds, subject to jurisdiction-specific rules and documentation requirements.

- **Herd Immunity Threshold**: The proportion of a population that must be immune to a disease (through vaccination or prior infection) to prevent sustained community transmission, varying by pathogen based on its transmissibility.

- **Immunization Information System (IIS)**: A confidential, population-based, computerized database that records all immunization doses administered by participating providers within a geographic area, enabling consolidated vaccination histories.

- **Minimum Interval**: The shortest acceptable time period between two consecutive doses of the same vaccine series, below which a dose is considered invalid and must be repeated.

- **Precaution**: A condition in the recipient that may increase the risk of an adverse reaction or reduce vaccine effectiveness, warranting a risk-benefit assessment before administration but not an absolute contraindication.

- **Primary Series**: The initial sequence of vaccine doses required to establish baseline immunity against a target disease, with specific dose counts and intervals defined by the recommended immunization schedule.

- **Recommended Immunization Schedule**: The evidence-based timetable published by ACIP specifying which vaccines should be administered at which ages, including minimum ages, minimum intervals, and catch-up guidance.

- **VAERS (Vaccine Adverse Event Reporting System)**: A national passive surveillance system co-managed by the CDC and FDA that accepts reports of adverse events following vaccination from healthcare providers, manufacturers, and the public.

- **Vaccine**: A biological preparation that stimulates the immune system to develop adaptive immunity against a specific pathogen, available in formulations including live attenuated, inactivated, subunit, mRNA, and viral vector types.

- **Vaccine Information Statement (VIS)**: A federally required document produced by the CDC that describes the benefits and risks of a vaccine, which must be provided to patients or their guardians before each vaccination.

- **Vaccine Injury Compensation Program (VICP)**: A federal no-fault program that provides financial compensation to individuals who suffer serious injuries caused by covered vaccines, as an alternative to litigation.

- **Vaccine Lot**: A homogeneous batch of a vaccine product manufactured under uniform conditions and assigned a unique lot number, enabling traceability from production through administration for recall and safety monitoring purposes.

- **Wastage**: Vaccine doses that are discarded without being administered to patients, due to expiration, cold chain failure, contamination, or remaining unused in opened multi-dose vials beyond their beyond-use date.
