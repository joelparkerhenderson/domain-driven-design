# Gynecology Domain - Ubiquitous Language

## Clinical Assessment Terms

- **Gynecological Examination**: A structured clinical assessment of the female reproductive system including inspection, palpation, and specimen collection.
- **Symptom Evaluation**: The systematic process of gathering, categorizing, and prioritizing patient-reported symptoms to guide diagnostic reasoning.
- **Diagnostic Workup**: An ordered sequence of laboratory tests, imaging studies, and procedures performed to confirm or exclude a suspected condition.
- **Clinical History**: A comprehensive record of a patient's past medical events, reproductive history, family history, and social factors relevant to gynecological care.
- **Differential Diagnosis**: A ranked list of candidate conditions that could explain the patient's presenting symptoms and examination findings.

## Reproductive Health Terms

- **Fertility Assessment**: An evaluation of ovarian reserve, ovulatory function, tubal patency, and partner factors to determine reproductive potential.
- **Contraception Plan**: A personalized strategy for preventing unintended pregnancy, selected based on patient preference, medical eligibility, and efficacy data.
- **Menstrual Disorder**: Any clinically significant deviation from normal menstrual patterns, including abnormal uterine bleeding, amenorrhea, and dysmenorrhea.
- **Ovulatory Cycle**: The recurring physiological process of follicular development, ovulation, and luteal phase that constitutes the menstrual cycle.
- **Hormonal Profile**: A set of laboratory measurements of reproductive hormones used to assess endocrine function and guide treatment decisions.

## Surgical Services Terms

- **Minimally Invasive Surgery**: Surgical techniques such as laparoscopy and hysteroscopy that use small incisions to reduce tissue trauma and recovery time.
- **Hysterectomy**: The surgical removal of the uterus, classified by approach (abdominal, vaginal, laparoscopic) and extent (total, subtotal, radical).
- **Pelvic Floor Repair**: Surgical correction of pelvic organ prolapse or urinary incontinence through reconstructive procedures.
- **Surgical Consent**: A documented agreement obtained after the patient has been informed of the procedure, risks, benefits, and alternatives.
- **Perioperative Protocol**: The standardized sequence of preoperative, intraoperative, and postoperative steps that govern safe surgical care.

## Prenatal Care Terms

- **Pregnancy Monitoring**: The scheduled series of clinical visits, laboratory tests, and imaging studies that track maternal and fetal health throughout gestation.
- **Ultrasound Assessment**: An imaging examination using sonography to evaluate fetal anatomy, growth, amniotic fluid volume, and placental position.
- **High-Risk Pregnancy**: A pregnancy complicated by maternal conditions, fetal anomalies, or obstetric history that requires intensified surveillance and specialist involvement.
- **Gestational Age**: The duration of pregnancy measured from the first day of the last menstrual period or confirmed by early ultrasound dating.
- **Labor Plan**: A clinical strategy for the anticipated mode, timing, and management of labor and delivery, developed collaboratively with the patient.

## Screening Programs Terms

- **Cervical Screening**: A preventive health program using Pap smears and HPV testing to detect precancerous cervical changes before they progress to invasive disease.
- **Breast Screening**: A structured program of clinical breast examination and mammography to detect breast cancer at an early, treatable stage.
- **STI Screening**: Laboratory-based testing for sexually transmitted infections, guided by patient risk factors, age, and clinical guidelines.
- **Screening Interval**: The recommended time period between successive screening episodes, determined by risk stratification and guideline protocols.
- **Abnormal Result Management**: The clinical pathway triggered when a screening test returns a result outside normal parameters, including follow-up testing, referral, and treatment.

## Patient Education Terms

- **Health Literacy Assessment**: An evaluation of a patient's ability to obtain, process, and understand basic health information needed to make informed decisions.
- **Shared Decision-Making**: A collaborative process in which clinicians and patients jointly select diagnostic or treatment options based on evidence and patient values.
- **Wellness Program**: A structured initiative that promotes preventive health behaviors such as nutrition, exercise, and routine screening adherence.
- **Informed Consent**: The process of providing a patient with sufficient information about a proposed intervention to enable a voluntary, knowledgeable decision.
- **Preventive Care Guidance**: Evidence-based recommendations for vaccinations, screenings, and lifestyle modifications that reduce gynecological disease risk.
