# Gerontology Domain

Domain-Driven Design (DDD) applied to gerontology and geriatric care systems.

## Overview

Gerontology is the scientific study of aging, encompassing biological, psychological, and social dimensions. Geriatric care applies this knowledge clinically to improve the health, function, and quality of life of older adults. This domain uses DDD principles to model the complex, interdisciplinary nature of geriatric care into bounded contexts that reflect real-world clinical workflows and organizational boundaries.

The domain addresses six major areas of geriatric care: comprehensive geriatric assessment, care coordination across interdisciplinary teams, cognitive health monitoring and dementia care, functional independence and assistive technology, medication management and polypharmacy reduction, and end-of-life planning including advance directives and palliative care.

## Bounded Contexts

1. **Geriatric Assessment Context** - Comprehensive geriatric assessment (CGA), frailty screening, fall risk evaluation, and multidimensional health status determination.

2. **Care Coordination Context** - Interdisciplinary team management, care transitions between settings, caregiver support programs, and community resource linkage.

3. **Cognitive Health Context** - Dementia screening using instruments such as MMSE and MoCA, cognitive decline tracking, and memory care planning.

4. **Functional Independence Context** - ADL and IADL assessment, mobility aids, home modification planning, and assistive technology management.

5. **Medication Management Context** - Polypharmacy review, Beers criteria application, deprescribing protocols, and medication adherence monitoring.

6. **End of Life Planning Context** - Advance directives, palliative care coordination, hospice referral, POLST orders, and goals-of-care conversations.
