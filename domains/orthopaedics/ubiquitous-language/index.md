# Orthopaedics Domain - Ubiquitous Language

- **Anterior Cruciate Ligament (ACL) Reconstruction**: A surgical procedure that replaces a torn ACL with a graft, typically autograft from the patellar tendon or hamstring, to restore knee stability and enable return to athletic activity.

- **Arthroplasty**: The surgical reconstruction or replacement of a damaged joint using prosthetic components, most commonly performed on the hip, knee, and shoulder to relieve pain and restore function.

- **Arthroscopy**: A minimally invasive surgical technique using a small camera and instruments inserted through small incisions to visualize, diagnose, and treat pathology within a joint.

- **AO/OTA Classification**: The standardized alphanumeric fracture classification system developed by the Arbeitsgemeinschaft fur Osteosynthesefragen (AO) Foundation and the Orthopaedic Trauma Association (OTA) that categorizes fractures by bone, segment, type, and complexity.

- **Closed Reduction**: A non-surgical procedure in which displaced bone fragments are realigned into anatomical position through external manipulation without making a surgical incision.

- **Degenerative Disc Disease**: A condition in which intervertebral discs lose hydration, height, and structural integrity over time, potentially causing pain, stiffness, and nerve compression in the spine.

- **External Fixation**: A surgical technique in which pins or wires are inserted through the skin into bone fragments and connected to an external frame to stabilize a fracture or correct a deformity.

- **Fracture**: A complete or partial break in the continuity of bone, classified by location, pattern, displacement, and involvement of the joint surface.

- **Fracture Union**: The biological process by which broken bone heals and regains structural integrity, assessed clinically by absence of pain on loading and radiographically by bridging callus formation.

- **Implant**: A manufactured prosthetic device surgically placed within the body to replace a joint surface, stabilize a fracture, or correct a deformity, made from materials such as titanium, cobalt-chromium, or polyethylene.

- **Internal Fixation**: The surgical stabilization of a fracture using implanted hardware such as plates, screws, intramedullary nails, or wires to maintain bone alignment during healing.

- **Laminectomy**: A surgical procedure in which part or all of the vertebral lamina is removed to relieve pressure on the spinal cord or nerve roots caused by spinal stenosis or disc herniation.

- **Meniscus**: A crescent-shaped fibrocartilage structure in the knee that acts as a shock absorber, load distributor, and joint stabilizer between the femoral condyle and tibial plateau.

- **Nonunion**: The failure of a fracture to heal within the expected timeframe, typically defined as absence of radiographic healing progression at six to nine months post-injury, requiring intervention.

- **Open Reduction and Internal Fixation (ORIF)**: A surgical procedure in which a fracture site is directly exposed through an incision, the bone fragments are reduced into anatomical alignment, and hardware is applied to maintain the reduction.

- **Osteotomy**: A planned surgical cut made in bone to correct alignment, redistribute weight-bearing forces, or facilitate deformity correction, commonly performed around the knee or hip.

- **Range of Motion (ROM)**: The measured arc of movement at a joint, expressed in degrees, assessed actively (patient-generated) or passively (examiner-generated) and compared to normative values.

- **Rotator Cuff**: A group of four muscles and their tendons (supraspinatus, infraspinatus, teres minor, subscapularis) that stabilize the glenohumeral joint and enable shoulder rotation and elevation.

- **Spinal Fusion**: A surgical procedure that permanently joins two or more vertebrae using bone graft and instrumentation to eliminate motion at a painful or unstable spinal segment.

- **Spinal Stenosis**: A narrowing of the spinal canal or neural foramina that compresses the spinal cord or nerve roots, causing pain, numbness, or weakness in the extremities.

- **Total Hip Replacement (THR)**: An arthroplasty procedure in which both the acetabular socket and the femoral head are replaced with prosthetic components to treat severe hip arthritis or fracture.

- **Total Knee Replacement (TKR)**: An arthroplasty procedure in which the damaged surfaces of the femoral condyles, tibial plateau, and optionally the patella are replaced with prosthetic components to treat severe knee arthritis.

- **Weight-Bearing Status**: A prescribed restriction or permission on the amount of body weight a patient may place through an injured or surgically repaired extremity, classified as non-weight-bearing, toe-touch, partial, weight-bearing as tolerated, or full weight-bearing.
