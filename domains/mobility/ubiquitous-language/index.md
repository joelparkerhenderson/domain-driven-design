# Mobility Domain - Ubiquitous Language

- **Accessibility Assessment**: A structured evaluation of a physical environment to identify architectural barriers, hazard risks, and modification opportunities that affect an individual's ability to move independently.

- **Ambulation**: The act of walking, including supported ambulation with assistive devices and independent ambulation without external aids.

- **Assistive Device**: Any equipment or product used to increase, maintain, or improve the functional mobility of a person with a physical limitation, including walkers, canes, crutches, wheelchairs, and scooters.

- **Balance**: The ability to maintain the body's center of mass over its base of support during static positions and dynamic movements, assessed using standardized scales such as the Berg Balance Scale.

- **Berg Balance Scale (BBS)**: A validated 14-item clinical assessment tool that measures static and dynamic balance ability on a scale of 0 to 56, with lower scores indicating greater fall risk.

- **Fall Event**: An unplanned descent to the floor, ground, or lower level that may or may not result in injury, documented with circumstances, contributing factors, and clinical consequences.

- **Fall Risk Screening**: A systematic process of identifying individuals at elevated risk for falling through validated tools, clinical history, medication review, and functional assessment.

- **Functional Independence Measure (FIM)**: A standardized 18-item assessment instrument that measures the level of assistance required for activities of daily living, scored from 1 (total assistance) to 7 (complete independence) per item.

- **Functional Mobility**: The ability to move from one position or place to another in a manner that allows participation in activities of daily living, encompassing bed mobility, transfers, ambulation, and stair negotiation.

- **Gait Analysis**: The systematic study of walking patterns through observation, instrumented measurement, or video analysis to identify deviations from normal gait and inform treatment planning.

- **Gait Deviation**: Any departure from a normal walking pattern in terms of timing, range of motion, force production, or coordination, potentially caused by musculoskeletal, neurological, or pain-related factors.

- **Home Modification**: A physical alteration to a residential environment designed to improve safety and accessibility, such as installation of grab bars, ramp construction, doorway widening, or bathroom renovation.

- **Mobility Aid Prescription**: A formal clinical order specifying the type, configuration, and features of an assistive device, supported by medical justification and functional assessment documentation.

- **Multifactorial Fall Intervention**: A coordinated set of actions addressing multiple identified fall risk factors simultaneously, typically including exercise, medication adjustment, vision correction, and environmental modification.

- **Orthotics**: Custom-fabricated or prefabricated external devices applied to the body to support, align, correct, or improve the function of movable body parts, commonly used at the ankle and foot (AFO) or knee (KAFO).

- **Spatiotemporal Gait Parameters**: Quantitative measures of walking that describe the timing and distance characteristics of gait, including step length, stride length, cadence, walking speed, and double support time.

- **Timed Up and Go (TUG) Test**: A validated clinical test that measures the time in seconds for a person to rise from a chair, walk three meters, turn, walk back, and sit down, used to assess functional mobility and fall risk.

- **Transfer**: The process of moving from one surface or position to another, such as bed to wheelchair, wheelchair to toilet, or sit to stand, evaluated for level of independence and safety.

- **Wheelchair Assessment**: A comprehensive evaluation to determine the most appropriate wheelchair type, seating system, cushion, and accessories based on the user's physical abilities, postural needs, and activity requirements.
