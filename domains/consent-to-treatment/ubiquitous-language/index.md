# Consent to Treatment - Ubiquitous Language

- **Advance Directive**: A written statement made by a person with capacity that specifies their treatment preferences or refusals to take effect if they later lose the ability to make or communicate decisions.

- **Best Interests**: The standard applied when making treatment decisions on behalf of a patient who lacks capacity, considering the patient's past and present wishes, beliefs, values, and all relevant circumstances.

- **Capacity**: The ability of a patient to understand, retain, and weigh information relevant to a treatment decision and to communicate that decision, assessed at the time consent is sought.

- **Clinician**: A healthcare professional who proposes, explains, or performs a treatment and who is responsible for obtaining valid consent from the patient or their proxy.

- **Coercion**: The use of pressure, threats, or undue influence that compromises the voluntariness of a patient's consent decision, rendering the consent invalid.

- **Consent Form**: A documented record capturing the patient's agreement or refusal to a proposed treatment, including the information disclosed, the patient's decision, and the signatures of the patient and clinician.

- **Disclosure**: The provision of material information to a patient about a proposed treatment, including its nature, purpose, risks, benefits, alternatives, and the consequences of not proceeding.

- **Emergency Authorization**: A clinical determination that treatment must proceed without explicit consent because delay would pose a serious risk to the patient's life or health and the patient cannot consent.

- **Gillick Competence**: A legal standard used in England and Wales to determine whether a child under sixteen has sufficient understanding and maturity to consent to a proposed treatment without parental involvement.

- **Implied Consent**: Consent inferred from a patient's conduct or circumstances, such as presenting an arm for a blood pressure measurement, rather than through an explicit verbal or written agreement.

- **Informed Consent**: A voluntary decision made by a patient who has received and understood adequate information about the nature, purpose, risks, benefits, and alternatives of a proposed treatment.

- **Lasting Power of Attorney (LPA)**: A legal instrument by which a person with capacity appoints another individual to make health and welfare decisions, including treatment decisions, on their behalf should they lose capacity.

- **Material Risk**: A risk that a reasonable person in the patient's position would consider significant when deciding whether to consent to a proposed treatment, or a risk that the clinician knows or should know this particular patient would consider significant.

- **Mental Capacity Act (MCA)**: The primary legislation in England and Wales (2005) that provides the legal framework for assessing capacity and making decisions on behalf of individuals who lack the capacity to make specific decisions.

- **Montgomery Duty**: The legal standard established by the UK Supreme Court in Montgomery v Lanarkshire (2015) requiring clinicians to disclose material risks that a reasonable patient would want to know.

- **Patient Autonomy**: The ethical principle that competent patients have the right to make their own treatment decisions, including the right to refuse treatment, even if the decision is considered unwise by their clinician.

- **Proxy**: A person legally authorized to make treatment decisions on behalf of a patient who lacks capacity, such as a lasting power of attorney holder, court-appointed deputy, or parent of a minor.

- **Refusal**: A competent patient's decision to decline a proposed treatment after receiving adequate information, which must be respected regardless of the clinical consequences.

- **Therapeutic Privilege**: A narrow and rarely invoked exception allowing a clinician to withhold information if disclosure would cause the patient serious harm, distinct from the clinician merely believing the patient would be upset.

- **Valid Consent**: Consent that meets all legal requirements: the patient has capacity, has received adequate disclosure of material information, and has made their decision voluntarily without coercion.

- **Voluntariness**: The requirement that a consent decision is made freely by the patient without undue influence, manipulation, or coercion from clinicians, family members, or other parties.

- **Withdrawal of Consent**: A patient's right to revoke previously given consent at any time before or during a treatment, which must be respected and documented by the treating clinician.
