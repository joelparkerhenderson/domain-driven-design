# Cognitive Science Domain - AGENTS.md

## Overview

This domain applies Domain-Driven Design (DDD) to cognitive science, encompassing perception and sensory processing, attention and executive function, memory and learning, language and communication, reasoning and decision-making, and consciousness and metacognition.

## Bounded Contexts

1. Perception and Sensory Processing - Manages the modeling of how organisms receive, organize, and interpret sensory information from the environment, including visual, auditory, tactile, olfactory, and gustatory processing.
2. Attention and Executive Function - Governs the cognitive mechanisms that control selective focus, sustained concentration, task switching, inhibitory control, and working memory management.
3. Memory Systems - Handles the encoding, storage, consolidation, and retrieval of information across different memory types including sensory, short-term, working, episodic, semantic, and procedural memory.
4. Language and Communication - Manages the cognitive processes underlying language acquisition, comprehension, production, and pragmatic use, spanning phonology, morphology, syntax, semantics, and discourse.
5. Reasoning and Decision-Making - Governs the cognitive mechanisms of logical reasoning, problem-solving, judgment under uncertainty, heuristics, biases, and rational and bounded-rational choice.
6. Cognitive Assessment - Manages the tools, methods, and frameworks used to measure and evaluate cognitive functions, including psychometric testing, neuroimaging interpretation, and computational modeling.

## Domain Principles

- Model using shared ubiquitous language between cognitive scientists, neuroscientists, psychologists, linguists, computer scientists, and philosophers of mind.
- Group the system into bounded contexts reflecting interdisciplinary cognitive science research and clinical workflows.
- Business logic remains pure and independent of infrastructure (database, UI, web, mobile).
- Follow established frameworks from the Cognitive Science Society, American Psychological Association, and international cognitive research standards.
- Maintain reproducibility, methodological rigor, and ethical research compliance as cross-cutting concerns.
