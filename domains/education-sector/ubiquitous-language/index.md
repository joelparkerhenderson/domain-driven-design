# Education Sector - Ubiquitous Language

- **Academic Standing**: A student's classification based on cumulative academic performance, such as good standing, academic probation, or academic dismissal, determined by institutional GPA thresholds and progression requirements.

- **Accreditation**: The formal recognition by an authorized external body that an institution or academic program meets established quality standards, typically involving self-study, peer review, and periodic reaffirmation.

- **Assessment**: A structured activity designed to measure student learning against defined learning outcomes, classified as formative (ongoing feedback) or summative (final evaluation contributing to a grade).

- **Course**: A discrete unit of instruction focused on a defined subject area, with specified learning outcomes, credit value, contact hours, assessment methods, and prerequisites.

- **Credit Hour**: A standardized unit of academic workload representing a specified number of hours of instruction and independent study, used to measure progress toward degree completion.

- **Curriculum**: The structured plan of study for an academic program, comprising the required and elective courses, credit requirements, prerequisite chains, and learning outcome expectations that lead to a qualification.

- **Degree Conferral**: The formal awarding of an academic qualification (such as a bachelor's, master's, or doctoral degree) to a student who has satisfied all program requirements, approved by the institution's governing body.

- **Enrollment**: The formal registration of a student in a specific course section for a given academic term, establishing the student's participation rights and assessment obligations.

- **Formative Assessment**: An ongoing assessment activity conducted during instruction that provides feedback to students and instructors for the purpose of improving learning, without contributing directly to a final grade.

- **Grade Point Average (GPA)**: A numerical measure of a student's academic performance calculated by dividing the total grade points earned by the total credit hours attempted, typically on a 4.0 scale.

- **Learning Management System (LMS)**: A software platform used to deliver, track, and manage instructional content, assignments, assessments, grades, and communication for courses and academic programs.

- **Learning Outcome**: A specific, measurable statement describing what a student is expected to know, understand, or be able to do upon successful completion of a course or program.

- **Matriculation**: The formal process by which a newly admitted student is officially enrolled and registered at an institution, establishing their student record and academic identity.

- **Moderation**: The quality assurance process by which assessment marks are reviewed by a second examiner or moderation panel to ensure consistency, fairness, and adherence to marking criteria.

- **Module**: A self-contained unit of study within a program, typically smaller than a full course, with defined learning outcomes, assessment tasks, and credit value, sometimes used interchangeably with course.

- **Prerequisite**: A course, skill, or requirement that a student must complete or satisfy before being permitted to enroll in a subsequent course, enforcing the sequencing of academic content.

- **Program**: A structured academic pathway leading to a formal qualification, comprising a defined set of required courses, electives, credit requirements, and progression rules.

- **Rubric**: A scoring guide that articulates the expectations for an assessment at various levels of performance, providing criteria and standards for consistent and transparent grading.

- **Semester**: A defined academic term, typically spanning fifteen to eighteen weeks, during which courses are delivered, assessments are conducted, and grades are recorded.

- **Student Record**: The official institutional record containing a student's personal information, enrollment history, course registrations, grades, academic standing, and degree progress.

- **Summative Assessment**: An assessment activity conducted at the end of an instructional period that evaluates student achievement against learning outcomes and contributes to a final grade or classification.

- **Transcript**: An official institutional document listing all courses attempted, grades earned, credit hours, cumulative GPA, degrees conferred, and academic honors for a student's entire enrollment history.
