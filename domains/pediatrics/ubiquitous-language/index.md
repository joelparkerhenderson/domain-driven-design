# Pediatrics Domain - Ubiquitous Language

- **Anticipatory Guidance**: Age-appropriate counseling provided to parents and caregivers during well-child visits about upcoming developmental changes, safety precautions, nutrition, sleep, and behavioral expectations.

- **Apgar Score**: A rapid clinical assessment performed at one and five minutes after birth that evaluates a newborn's heart rate, respiratory effort, muscle tone, reflex irritability, and color on a scale of 0 to 10.

- **Bright Futures Periodicity Schedule**: The American Academy of Pediatrics framework that specifies the recommended timing and content of preventive health visits, screenings, and assessments from birth through age twenty-one.

- **Catch-Up Immunization Schedule**: An accelerated vaccination plan for children who have missed one or more recommended doses, specifying minimum intervals and ages to bring the patient current with the routine immunization schedule.

- **Developmental Milestone**: A specific skill or behavior that most children can perform by a certain age, used as markers to monitor progress in motor, language, cognitive, and social-emotional domains.

- **Developmental Screening**: A brief, standardized assessment tool administered at specified ages to identify children who may be at risk for developmental delays and who require further diagnostic evaluation.

- **Failure to Thrive (FTT)**: A clinical designation for inadequate physical growth in infants and children, typically defined as weight below the third percentile or a significant downward crossing of growth chart percentile lines.

- **Growth Chart**: A standardized graph that plots a child's anthropometric measurements (weight, height, head circumference, BMI) against age- and sex-specific population reference data to track growth patterns over time.

- **Growth Percentile**: A statistical measure indicating where a child's measurement falls relative to a reference population, expressing the percentage of children of the same age and sex who have a lower value.

- **HEEADSSS Assessment**: A structured psychosocial screening framework for adolescents that evaluates Home environment, Education and employment, Eating, peer Activities, Drugs, Sexuality, Suicide and depression risk, and Safety.

- **Immunization Information System (IIS)**: A confidential, population-based, computerized database maintained by state or regional public health agencies that records all immunization doses administered to individuals residing within a jurisdiction.

- **Newborn Screening**: A public health program that tests all newborns for a panel of serious but treatable genetic, metabolic, and endocrine disorders, typically performed via heel-prick blood spot collection within 24 to 48 hours of birth.

- **Pediatric Dosing**: The calculation of medication doses based on the child's body weight (mg/kg) or body surface area (mg/m2), accounting for age-related differences in drug metabolism, distribution, and clearance.

- **Pediatric Patient**: An individual from birth through age eighteen who receives medical care within the pediatric healthcare system, with clinical management adapted to their developmental stage and age-specific physiology.

- **Vaccine Adverse Event Reporting System (VAERS)**: A national surveillance program co-managed by the CDC and FDA that collects reports of adverse events occurring after vaccination to monitor vaccine safety.

- **Vaccine Information Statement (VIS)**: A federally required document provided to patients or parents before each vaccine dose that explains the benefits and risks of the vaccine being administered.

- **Well-Child Visit**: A scheduled preventive healthcare encounter for pediatric patients that includes physical examination, growth measurement, developmental assessment, immunization administration, and anticipatory guidance.

- **Weight-for-Age Percentile**: A growth indicator that compares a child's weight to the reference population of children of the same age and sex, used to identify underweight, healthy weight, or overweight status.
