# Medical Records Release Permission Domain - Ubiquitous Language

- **Accounting of Disclosures**: A record that HIPAA requires covered entities to maintain and make available to patients upon request, documenting each disclosure of their protected health information made for purposes other than treatment, payment, or healthcare operations.

- **Authorization**: A written, signed document from a patient or their legal representative granting specific permission for a covered entity to use or disclose their protected health information for a stated purpose, to a named recipient, within a defined time period.

- **Breach**: An impermissible use or disclosure of protected health information that compromises the security or privacy of the information, as defined under the HIPAA Breach Notification Rule, unless a risk assessment demonstrates a low probability of compromise.

- **Business Associate**: A person or organization that performs functions or activities on behalf of a covered entity that involve the use or disclosure of protected health information, bound by a business associate agreement to comply with HIPAA requirements.

- **Covered Entity**: A health plan, healthcare clearinghouse, or healthcare provider that transmits health information electronically and is therefore subject to HIPAA Privacy and Security Rules.

- **De-Identification**: The process of removing or transforming specified identifiers from protected health information so that the resulting data cannot reasonably be used to identify an individual, as defined by HIPAA's Safe Harbor or Expert Determination methods.

- **Designated Record Set**: The group of records maintained by or for a covered entity that includes medical records, billing records, enrollment records, and any other records used to make decisions about individuals, to which patients have a right of access.

- **Disclosure**: The release, transfer, provision of access to, or divulging of protected health information to any person or entity outside the covered entity that maintains the information.

- **Health Information Exchange (HIE)**: The electronic sharing of health-related information among organizations according to nationally recognized standards, enabling authorized providers and patients to access and securely share medical information.

- **HIPAA Privacy Rule**: The federal regulation (45 CFR Part 164, Subparts A and E) that establishes national standards for the protection of individually identifiable health information, defining permitted uses and disclosures and patient rights.

- **Informed Consent**: The process by which a patient receives adequate information about the nature, scope, and consequences of authorizing the release of their health information, enabling a voluntary and knowledgeable decision.

- **Minimum Necessary Standard**: The HIPAA requirement that covered entities limit the use, disclosure, and request of protected health information to the minimum amount reasonably necessary to accomplish the intended purpose.

- **Patient Portal**: A secure online platform that provides patients with electronic access to their health information and the ability to manage authorizations, request records, and view disclosure history.

- **Personal Representative**: A person authorized under applicable law to act on behalf of a patient in making healthcare decisions, including a parent of a minor, a legal guardian, or a person with healthcare power of attorney.

- **Protected Health Information (PHI)**: Individually identifiable health information held or transmitted by a covered entity or its business associate, in any form or medium, that relates to past, present, or future health conditions, treatment, or payment.

- **Purpose of Use**: The stated reason for which protected health information is being requested or disclosed, such as treatment, payment, healthcare operations, legal proceedings, public health, or research.

- **Release Request**: A formal submission from an authorized requestor asking a covered entity to disclose specified protected health information, which must be validated against an existing patient authorization or a permitted use exception.

- **Requestor**: An individual or entity that submits a request for disclosure of protected health information, such as the patient, another healthcare provider, an attorney, an insurance company, or a government agency.

- **Revocation**: The act by a patient or their personal representative of withdrawing a previously granted authorization for the release of protected health information, effective upon receipt by the covered entity except to the extent that action has already been taken in reliance on the authorization.

- **Sensitive Information Category**: A classification of protected health information that receives heightened privacy protections under federal or state law, including substance abuse treatment records (42 CFR Part 2), mental health records, HIV/AIDS status, and genetic information.

- **Verification**: The process by which a covered entity confirms the identity and authority of a person requesting protected health information before processing the disclosure, as required by the HIPAA Privacy Rule.
