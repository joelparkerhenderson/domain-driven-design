# Stroke Domain - Ubiquitous Language

- **ASPECTS (Alberta Stroke Program Early CT Score)**: A 10-point scoring system applied to non-contrast CT imaging that quantifies the extent of early ischemic changes in the middle cerebral artery territory, with lower scores indicating larger infarct burden.

- **Alteplase**: A recombinant tissue plasminogen activator (tPA) administered intravenously to dissolve blood clots in acute ischemic stroke, approved for use within 4.5 hours of symptom onset in eligible patients.

- **Carotid Stenosis**: Narrowing of the carotid arteries due to atherosclerotic plaque, a significant risk factor for ischemic stroke that may require surgical endarterectomy or stenting for secondary prevention.

- **CT Angiography (CTA)**: A contrast-enhanced imaging study that visualizes the cerebral vasculature to identify large vessel occlusions, arterial dissections, and stenoses in the acute stroke evaluation.

- **Door-to-Needle Time**: The elapsed time from a stroke patient's arrival at the emergency department to the initiation of intravenous thrombolysis, with a guideline target of 60 minutes or less.

- **Door-to-Puncture Time**: The elapsed time from a stroke patient's arrival at the emergency department to the first arterial puncture for mechanical thrombectomy, with a guideline target of 90 minutes or less.

- **Dysphagia Screening**: A bedside swallowing assessment performed before a stroke patient is given any food, liquid, or oral medications, to detect aspiration risk and prevent pneumonia.

- **Hemorrhagic Stroke**: A type of stroke caused by the rupture of a blood vessel in the brain, resulting in intracerebral hemorrhage or subarachnoid hemorrhage, requiring different management than ischemic stroke.

- **Ischemic Stroke**: A type of stroke caused by the obstruction of blood flow to the brain due to a thrombus or embolus, accounting for approximately 87 percent of all stroke cases.

- **Large Vessel Occlusion (LVO)**: A blockage of a major intracranial artery such as the internal carotid or proximal middle cerebral artery, potentially amenable to mechanical thrombectomy in eligible patients.

- **Last Known Well Time**: The most recent time at which the patient was observed to be at their neurological baseline, used to calculate treatment eligibility when the exact symptom onset is unknown.

- **Mechanical Thrombectomy**: An endovascular procedure in which a catheter-based device is used to physically remove a blood clot from a large intracranial artery, indicated within up to 24 hours of last known well in selected patients.

- **Modified Rankin Scale (mRS)**: A 7-point disability outcome scale ranging from 0 (no symptoms) to 6 (death), used to measure the degree of functional independence after stroke at 90-day follow-up.

- **NIHSS (National Institutes of Health Stroke Scale)**: A 15-item standardized neurological examination scored from 0 to 42 that quantifies stroke severity by assessing consciousness, gaze, visual fields, motor function, sensation, language, and neglect.

- **Penumbra**: The region of brain tissue surrounding the ischemic core that is hypoperfused but still potentially salvageable if blood flow is restored in a timely manner, identified through perfusion imaging.

- **Perfusion Imaging**: CT or MRI imaging that measures cerebral blood flow parameters to identify the ischemic core and salvageable penumbra, used to guide treatment decisions in extended time windows.

- **Secondary Prevention**: The set of medical interventions and lifestyle modifications implemented after a stroke to reduce the risk of recurrence, including antiplatelet therapy, anticoagulation, statins, and blood pressure control.

- **Stroke Code**: An emergency activation protocol triggered when a suspected stroke patient is identified, mobilizing a coordinated response from emergency medicine, neurology, radiology, and interventional teams.

- **Stroke Unit**: A specialized hospital ward with trained nursing staff, continuous monitoring capabilities, and organized multidisciplinary care protocols dedicated to the acute management of stroke patients.

- **Telestroke**: A telemedicine service enabling remote neurological consultation via video for stroke patients presenting to hospitals without on-site stroke specialist coverage, allowing timely treatment decisions.

- **Tenecteplase**: A modified tissue plasminogen activator with a longer half-life than alteplase, increasingly used as a single-bolus alternative for intravenous thrombolysis in acute ischemic stroke.

- **Transient Ischemic Attack (TIA)**: A temporary episode of neurological dysfunction caused by focal brain ischemia without acute infarction, considered an urgent warning sign for impending stroke requiring immediate evaluation.
