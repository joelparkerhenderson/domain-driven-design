# Prenatal Domain - Ubiquitous Language

- **Amniocentesis**: A diagnostic procedure in which a sample of amniotic fluid is withdrawn from the uterus through a needle to test for chromosomal abnormalities, genetic disorders, and fetal lung maturity.

- **Biophysical Profile (BPP)**: A prenatal assessment that combines ultrasound evaluation of fetal movement, tone, breathing, and amniotic fluid volume with a non-stress test to assess fetal well-being.

- **Birth Plan**: A document expressing the expectant mother's preferences for labor and delivery, including pain management choices, delivery position, and immediate postpartum care wishes.

- **Cell-Free DNA Testing**: A non-invasive prenatal screening method that analyzes fragments of fetal DNA circulating in the maternal bloodstream to assess the risk of chromosomal conditions such as trisomy 21, 18, and 13.

- **Chorionic Villus Sampling (CVS)**: A diagnostic procedure performed in the first trimester in which a sample of placental tissue is obtained to test for chromosomal abnormalities and genetic conditions.

- **Doppler Velocimetry**: An ultrasound technique that measures blood flow velocity in fetal and maternal vessels to assess placental function and detect conditions such as intrauterine growth restriction.

- **Estimated Date of Delivery (EDD)**: The projected date of birth calculated from the first day of the last menstrual period or from early ultrasound measurements, serving as the reference point for gestational age.

- **Fetal Biometry**: A set of ultrasound measurements including biparietal diameter, head circumference, abdominal circumference, and femur length used to estimate fetal weight and assess growth adequacy.

- **Gestational Age**: The duration of pregnancy measured in weeks and days from the first day of the last menstrual period, serving as the primary temporal framework for scheduling all prenatal assessments and interventions.

- **Gestational Diabetes**: A form of glucose intolerance first identified during pregnancy, typically screened for between 24 and 28 weeks of gestation using an oral glucose tolerance test.

- **Gravida**: The total number of times a woman has been pregnant, regardless of outcome, used in obstetric history documentation alongside parity.

- **Group B Streptococcus (GBS) Screening**: A vaginal and rectal culture performed between 36 and 37 weeks of gestation to detect GBS colonization and determine the need for intrapartum antibiotic prophylaxis.

- **High-Risk Pregnancy**: A pregnancy in which the mother, fetus, or both face an elevated likelihood of complications due to pre-existing medical conditions, obstetric history, or findings identified during prenatal care.

- **Non-Stress Test (NST)**: A prenatal monitoring procedure that records fetal heart rate patterns in response to fetal movement to assess the adequacy of fetal oxygenation.

- **Nuchal Translucency**: An ultrasound measurement of the fluid-filled space at the back of the fetal neck performed in the first trimester, used as a screening marker for chromosomal abnormalities including Down syndrome.

- **Parity**: The number of pregnancies a woman has carried to a viable gestational age, recorded as part of the obstetric history using the GTPAL system (gravida, term, preterm, abortions, living).

- **Preeclampsia**: A pregnancy-specific hypertensive disorder characterized by new-onset high blood pressure and proteinuria or other end-organ dysfunction after 20 weeks of gestation.

- **Prenatal Visit**: A scheduled clinical encounter between a pregnant patient and her care provider for routine monitoring, screening, and counseling according to a gestational age-based schedule.

- **Quad Screen**: A second-trimester maternal serum screening test measuring four analytes (AFP, hCG, estriol, inhibin A) to assess the risk of Down syndrome, trisomy 18, and neural tube defects.

- **Rhesus (Rh) Factor**: A blood group antigen whose incompatibility between an Rh-negative mother and an Rh-positive fetus can cause hemolytic disease of the newborn, prevented by administering Rh immunoglobulin (RhoGAM).

- **Trimester**: One of three approximately equal periods into which pregnancy is divided, each spanning roughly 13 weeks and associated with distinct developmental milestones and clinical care priorities.

- **Ultrasound**: A diagnostic imaging modality using high-frequency sound waves to visualize the fetus, placenta, and amniotic fluid, used for dating, anatomy surveys, growth assessment, and procedural guidance.
