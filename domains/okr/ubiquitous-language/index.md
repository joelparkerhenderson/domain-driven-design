# Objectives and Key Results - Ubiquitous Language

Objective: A qualitative, inspirational goal that describes what an organization, team, or individual wants to achieve.

Key Result: A quantitative, measurable outcome that indicates progress toward an objective.

OKR Cycle: A time-bounded period (typically quarterly) during which objectives and key results are pursued.

Alignment: The process of ensuring OKRs at different organizational levels support and reinforce each other.

Cascading: The practice of deriving lower-level OKRs from higher-level objectives to maintain strategic coherence.

Check-In: A regular progress review meeting where key result status is updated and obstacles discussed.

Scoring: The evaluation of key result achievement, typically on a 0.0 to 1.0 scale.

Stretch Goal: An ambitious target designed to push performance beyond comfortable achievement levels.

Confidence Level: A subjective assessment of the likelihood of achieving a key result by the end of the cycle.

Owner: The individual or team accountable for driving progress on a specific objective or key result.

Contributor: A person or team that supports progress on a key result without being the primary owner.

SMART: Specific, Measurable, Actionable, Relatable, Timely — criteria for well-formed goals.

KPI: Key Performance Indicator — an ongoing metric that measures operational health or business performance.

KRI: Key Risk Indicator — a metric that signals increasing risk exposure requiring attention.

CSF: Critical Success Factor — a condition that must be met for a strategy or project to succeed.

CTP: Critical To Process — a measurable characteristic of a process that must be controlled to ensure quality.

CTQ: Critical To Quality — a measurable characteristic important to the customer or stakeholder.

OGSM: Objectives, Goals, Strategies, Measures — a strategic planning framework that cascades from vision to metrics.

GIST: Goals, Ideas, Steps, Tasks — a lightweight planning framework for translating goals into actionable tasks.

OODA: Observe, Orient, Decide, Act — a decision-making loop for rapid situational assessment and response.

PDCA: Plan, Do, Check, Act — a continuous improvement cycle for iterative process refinement.

DMAIC: Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control — a Six Sigma methodology for process improvement.

QOC: Questions, Options, Criteria — a decision-making framework for evaluating alternatives against criteria.

SWOT: Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats — a strategic analysis framework for situational assessment.

PEST: Political, Economic, Social, Technological — a macro-environmental analysis framework.

RAID: Risks, Assumptions, Issues, Dependencies — a project management tracking framework.

RICE: Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort — a prioritization scoring framework for evaluating initiatives.

ADDIE: Analyze, Design, Develop, Implement, Evaluate — an instructional design framework.

ADKAR: Awareness, Desire, Knowledge, Ability, Reinforcement — a change management model.

ITIL: Information Technology Infrastructure Library — a framework for IT service management best practices.

PERT: Program Evaluation Review Technique — a project scheduling method using probabilistic time estimates.

SOP: Standard Operating Procedure — a documented set of instructions for routine operations.

BOK: Book Of Knowledge — a comprehensive reference of standards and practices for a professional discipline.

DR: Decision Record — a documented record capturing the context, options considered, and rationale for a decision.

Initiative: A project or workstream that contributes to achieving one or more key results.

Cadence: The recurring rhythm of OKR-related activities (setting, check-ins, scoring, retrospectives).

Retrospective: A review session at the end of an OKR cycle to assess what worked and what to improve.

Grading: The final assessment of key result achievement at the end of a cycle, informing learning and future planning.

Health Metric: An operational measure that must remain within acceptable bounds, distinct from aspirational OKR targets.

Moonshot: A highly ambitious objective with a low probability of full achievement, designed to drive breakthrough thinking.

Roofshot: A realistically achievable objective that pushes incremental improvement.

Committed OKR: An OKR with a high expectation of full achievement, often tied to business commitments.

Aspirational OKR: An OKR with intentional stretch, where achieving 70% is considered success.

Parent Objective: A higher-level objective from which child objectives are derived through cascading.

Child Objective: A lower-level objective that supports and contributes to a parent objective.

Progress Update: A periodic status report on key result advancement, including metrics and narrative.

Blocker: An obstacle preventing progress on a key result that requires escalation or intervention.
