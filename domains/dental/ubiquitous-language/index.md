# Dental Domain - Ubiquitous Language

Amalgam: A metallic restorative material composed of mercury, silver, tin, and copper used for direct dental fillings.
Bitewing Radiograph: An intraoral X-ray that captures the crowns of upper and lower teeth simultaneously to detect interproximal caries.
Bracket: An orthodontic attachment bonded to a tooth surface that holds an archwire to guide tooth movement.
Bridge: A fixed prosthetic device that replaces one or more missing teeth by anchoring to adjacent natural teeth or implants.
Caries: Bacterial-driven demineralization of tooth structure commonly known as tooth decay or cavities.
Case Presentation: A structured communication of diagnosis, treatment options, costs, and expected outcomes to a patient.
Claim: A formal request submitted to a dental insurance carrier for reimbursement of services rendered.
Composite Resin: A tooth-colored restorative material used for direct fillings that bonds to tooth structure.
Crown: A full-coverage restoration that encases the visible portion of a tooth to restore form and function.
Current Dental Terminology (CDT): The standardized code set maintained by the ADA for reporting dental procedures on insurance claims.
Dental Charting: A systematic graphical record of existing conditions, restorations, and pathology for each tooth.
Endodontics: The branch of dentistry concerned with the diagnosis and treatment of dental pulp and periapical disease.
Gingivitis: Inflammation of the gingival tissue that represents the earliest reversible stage of periodontal disease.
Implant: A titanium fixture surgically placed in the jawbone to serve as an artificial tooth root for prosthetic attachment.
Informed Consent: A documented agreement in which a patient acknowledges understanding of proposed treatment risks and alternatives.
Malocclusion: A deviation from ideal occlusal relationships classified by Angle's system into Class I, II, or III categories.
Occlusion: The relationship between the upper and lower teeth when the jaws are closed in functional contact.
Orthodontic Retention: The phase following active orthodontic treatment that uses retainers to maintain achieved tooth positions.
Panoramic Radiograph: An extraoral X-ray that produces a single image of the entire maxillofacial complex.
Patient Recall: A systematic process for scheduling patients for periodic preventive care and examination appointments.
Periapical Radiograph: An intraoral X-ray that shows the entire tooth from crown to root apex and surrounding bone.
Periodontal Pocket: A pathologically deepened sulcus between the tooth and gingiva measuring greater than three millimeters.
Periodontal Probing: The clinical measurement of sulcus or pocket depths around each tooth using a calibrated probe.
Prophylaxis: A professional dental cleaning procedure that removes plaque, calculus, and stains from tooth surfaces.
Root Canal Therapy: An endodontic procedure that removes infected pulp tissue, disinfects, and obturates the root canal system.
Scaling and Root Planing: A non-surgical periodontal procedure that removes subgingival calculus and smooths root surfaces.
Treatment Plan: A sequenced schedule of proposed dental procedures with associated timelines, priorities, and cost estimates.
Treatment Sequencing: The clinical ordering of procedures based on urgency, prerequisite dependencies, and healing requirements.
Veneer: A thin shell of porcelain or composite bonded to the facial surface of a tooth for aesthetic improvement.
Xerostomia: A condition of abnormally reduced salivary flow that increases caries risk and affects oral comfort.
