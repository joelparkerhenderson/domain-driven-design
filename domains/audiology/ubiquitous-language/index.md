# Audiology Domain - Ubiquitous Language

Audiogram: A graphical representation of hearing thresholds at specific frequencies, serving as the primary diagnostic record of a patient's hearing ability.

Audiometry: The measurement of an individual's hearing ability across frequencies and intensities using calibrated equipment.

Tympanometry: An objective test that measures the compliance and mobility of the tympanic membrane and middle ear system to detect conductive pathology.

Otoacoustic Emissions (OAE): Sounds produced by the outer hair cells of the cochlea, measured to assess cochlear function, particularly in newborn screening.

Auditory Brainstem Response (ABR): An electrophysiological test that records neural activity along the auditory pathway from the cochlea to the brainstem.

Speech Recognition Threshold (SRT): The lowest intensity level at which a patient can correctly repeat spondaic words fifty percent of the time.

Word Recognition Score (WRS): The percentage of phonetically balanced words a patient correctly identifies at a suprathreshold presentation level.

Pure Tone Average (PTA): The arithmetic mean of hearing thresholds at 500 Hz, 1000 Hz, and 2000 Hz, used to summarize hearing sensitivity.

Hearing Aid: An electroacoustic device that amplifies sound to compensate for hearing loss, available in various form factors and technology levels.

Cochlear Implant: A surgically implanted device that bypasses damaged hair cells and directly stimulates the auditory nerve to provide sound perception.

Real Ear Measurement (REM): Probe microphone verification of hearing aid output in the ear canal to confirm the device meets prescriptive targets.

Fitting Prescription: A calculated amplification target derived from audiometric data using validated formulae such as NAL-NL2 or DSL v5.

Aural Rehabilitation: A comprehensive program of interventions designed to improve communication abilities for individuals with hearing loss.

Auditory Training: Structured exercises that develop a patient's ability to perceive and process auditory signals, often used post-implantation.

Communication Strategy: Techniques taught to patients and communication partners to optimize understanding in challenging listening environments.

Vestibular Assessment: A battery of tests evaluating the balance system, including the semicircular canals, otolith organs, and central vestibular pathways.

Videonystagmography (VNG): A test using infrared video goggles to record eye movements and diagnose vestibular disorders by observing nystagmus patterns.

Vestibular Rehabilitation Therapy (VRT): An exercise-based program designed to promote central nervous system compensation for vestibular deficits.

Newborn Hearing Screening: A universal screening protocol using OAE or automated ABR to identify hearing loss within the first month of life.

Developmental Milestone: An age-appropriate auditory, speech, or language benchmark used to monitor pediatric patients with hearing loss.

Audiologic Report: A formal clinical document summarizing test results, diagnoses, recommendations, and treatment plans for a patient encounter.

Referral: A formal request from one clinician or context to another for evaluation, treatment, or consultation regarding a patient's condition.

Outcome Measure: A standardized instrument or metric used to quantify the effectiveness of an audiologic intervention over time.

Decibel Hearing Level (dB HL): The unit of measurement for hearing thresholds, calibrated relative to normative hearing sensitivity at each frequency.

Frequency: The pitch of a sound measured in Hertz (Hz), with audiometric testing typically spanning 250 Hz to 8000 Hz.

Air Conduction: Sound transmission through the outer ear, middle ear, and inner ear, tested with headphones or insert earphones.

Bone Conduction: Sound transmission that bypasses the outer and middle ear by vibrating the skull directly, used to isolate sensorineural hearing loss.

Masking: The introduction of noise into the non-test ear to prevent cross-hearing and ensure test results reflect the ear under evaluation.

Degree of Hearing Loss: A classification of hearing sensitivity ranging from normal to profound, based on pure tone average thresholds.

Hearing Loss Type: The classification of hearing impairment as conductive, sensorineural, or mixed, determined by comparing air and bone conduction thresholds.
