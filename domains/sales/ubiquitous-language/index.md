# Sales Domain - Ubiquitous Language

Lead: A potential customer who has shown initial interest in the company's products or services but has not yet been qualified.
Prospect: A lead that has been evaluated and meets the criteria for further sales engagement.
Qualification: The process of evaluating a lead against predefined criteria such as budget, authority, need, and timeline (BANT) to determine sales-readiness.
Lead Score: A numerical value assigned to a lead based on demographic fit and behavioral engagement signals.
Marketing Campaign: A coordinated set of activities designed to generate awareness and capture leads from a target market segment.
Opportunity: A qualified prospect with a specific potential deal that has entered the sales pipeline.
Deal: A potential transaction being negotiated between a sales representative and a prospect, tracked through pipeline stages.
Proposal: A formal document presenting the product or service offering, pricing, and terms to a prospect.
Win: A successfully closed deal where the prospect has committed to purchasing.
Loss: A deal that has been closed without a purchase, typically with a recorded reason for the outcome.
Sales Pipeline: The structured sequence of stages that an opportunity progresses through from initial contact to closed deal.
Pipeline Stage: A defined phase in the sales process such as prospecting, discovery, proposal, negotiation, or closed.
Forecasting: The process of predicting future sales revenue based on pipeline data, historical trends, and probability analysis.
Sales Velocity: A metric measuring the speed at which deals move through the pipeline, calculated from deal count, average value, win rate, and cycle length.
Order: A confirmed commitment from a customer to purchase specific products or services at agreed-upon terms.
Order Line: An individual item within an order specifying the product, quantity, unit price, and any applicable discounts.
Fulfillment Handoff: The event when a confirmed order is transferred from the sales domain to the fulfillment domain for processing and delivery.
Account: A business entity or individual that has an established relationship with the company, potentially encompassing multiple contacts and opportunities.
Contact: An individual person associated with an account who participates in or influences purchasing decisions.
Contract: A legally binding agreement between the company and an account specifying terms, pricing, duration, and obligations.
Renewal: The process of extending or re-establishing a contract at or before its expiration date.
Upsell: The practice of offering an existing customer a higher-tier or more feature-rich version of their current product or service.
Cross-sell: The practice of offering an existing customer complementary products or services that enhance their current purchase.
Sales Representative: A team member responsible for managing leads, opportunities, and accounts through the sales process.
Territory: A defined segment of the market, typically organized by geography, industry, or account size, assigned to a sales representative.
Quota: A target revenue or deal volume assigned to a sales representative or team for a specific time period.
Conversion Rate: The percentage of leads or opportunities that successfully advance to the next stage or close as wins.
Customer Lifetime Value: The projected total revenue a customer account is expected to generate over the entire duration of the business relationship.
Churn: The loss of a customer account due to contract non-renewal, cancellation, or competitive displacement.
Sales Cycle: The average elapsed time from initial lead capture to deal closure, measured across a cohort of closed opportunities.
Dashboard: A visual reporting interface that aggregates key sales metrics and performance indicators for decision-making.
