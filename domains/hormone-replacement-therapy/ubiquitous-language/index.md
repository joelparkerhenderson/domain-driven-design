# Hormone Replacement Therapy Domain - Ubiquitous Language

Hormone Replacement Therapy (HRT): Medical treatment that supplements or replaces hormones the body no longer produces in sufficient quantities due to menopause, hypogonadism, or other endocrine conditions.

Clinical Assessment: Structured evaluation of a patient's symptoms, medical history, hormone levels, and risk factors to determine candidacy for hormone replacement therapy.

Symptom Score: Quantified measurement of patient-reported symptoms using validated instruments such as the Menopause Rating Scale or the Kupperman Index.

Hormone Panel: Set of laboratory tests measuring circulating hormone levels including estradiol, progesterone, testosterone, FSH, LH, DHEA-S, and thyroid hormones.

Candidacy Screening: Formal evaluation process that determines whether a patient is eligible for HRT based on clinical criteria, contraindications, and risk-benefit analysis.

Treatment Protocol: Defined regimen specifying the hormone formulation, dose, delivery method, schedule, and duration for an individual patient.

Delivery Method: Route of administration for hormone therapy including transdermal patches, oral tablets, topical gels, subcutaneous pellets, intramuscular injections, and vaginal preparations.

Dose Titration: Systematic adjustment of hormone dosage based on laboratory results, symptom response, and side effect profile to achieve therapeutic targets.

Estrogen Therapy: Administration of estrogen (typically estradiol or conjugated equine estrogens) to address estrogen deficiency symptoms.

Progesterone Therapy: Administration of progesterone or progestins, often combined with estrogen therapy in patients with an intact uterus to protect the endometrium.

Testosterone Therapy: Administration of testosterone to address androgen deficiency, applicable to both male hypogonadism and female androgen insufficiency.

Bioidentical Hormones: Hormone preparations that are chemically identical to the hormones naturally produced by the human body.

Compounded Hormones: Custom-mixed hormone preparations created by compounding pharmacies to meet individual patient specifications.

Lab Monitoring: Scheduled laboratory testing to measure hormone levels, metabolic markers, and safety parameters during active HRT treatment.

Therapeutic Target Range: Clinically defined range of acceptable hormone levels that indicates adequate treatment response without excessive dosing.

Metabolic Panel: Set of laboratory tests evaluating liver function, lipid profile, glucose metabolism, and other metabolic parameters relevant to HRT safety.

Bone Density Assessment: Measurement of bone mineral density, typically via DEXA scan, to evaluate osteoporosis risk and treatment response.

Cardiovascular Risk Marker: Laboratory or clinical measurement used to assess cardiovascular safety during HRT, including lipid fractions, inflammatory markers, and coagulation factors.

Adverse Event: Any undesirable clinical occurrence temporally associated with hormone replacement therapy, regardless of causal relationship.

Contraindication: Medical condition or factor that makes a specific hormone therapy inadvisable due to elevated risk of harm.

Risk Stratification: Process of categorizing patients into risk tiers based on clinical factors to guide treatment decisions and monitoring intensity.

Formulation Adjustment: Change to the specific hormone preparation, concentration, or delivery system without altering the fundamental treatment intent.

Combination Therapy: Use of two or more hormones simultaneously, such as estrogen plus progesterone, or estrogen plus testosterone.

Symptom Resolution: Measurable improvement or elimination of presenting symptoms following initiation or adjustment of hormone therapy.

Quality of Life Score: Standardized measurement of patient well-being across physical, emotional, and functional domains using validated assessment tools.

Treatment Efficacy: Degree to which a hormone replacement therapy protocol achieves its intended clinical outcomes within a defined time frame.

Monitoring Schedule: Predefined timeline for laboratory tests and clinical follow-up visits based on treatment phase and patient risk profile.

Washout Period: Interval during which a patient discontinues a previous hormone treatment before transitioning to a new regimen, allowing prior hormones to clear the system.

Endocrine Feedback Loop: Physiological mechanism by which circulating hormone levels regulate the release of upstream hormones from the hypothalamus and pituitary gland.

Treatment Outcome: Aggregate result of hormone replacement therapy measured across symptom improvement, laboratory normalization, and long-term health impact.
