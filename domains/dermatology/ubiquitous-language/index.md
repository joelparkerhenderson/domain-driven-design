# Dermatology Domain - Ubiquitous Language

Dermoscopy: A non-invasive diagnostic technique using a dermatoscope to examine skin lesions at magnified scale, revealing subsurface structures not visible to the naked eye.

Lesion: An area of abnormal skin tissue identified during clinical examination, characterized by morphological descriptors and tracked through its diagnostic and treatment lifecycle.

Biopsy: The removal of a tissue sample from a skin lesion for histopathological examination to establish a definitive diagnosis.

Excision: The surgical removal of an entire skin lesion along with a margin of surrounding normal tissue, performed for both diagnostic and therapeutic purposes.

Mohs Surgery: A specialized micrographic surgical technique that removes skin cancer layer by layer, examining each layer microscopically until only cancer-free tissue remains, maximizing tissue preservation.

Cryotherapy: A treatment modality that destroys abnormal skin tissue by applying extreme cold, typically liquid nitrogen, to freeze and eliminate targeted lesions.

Phototherapy: The use of controlled ultraviolet light (UVB or PUVA) to treat chronic skin conditions such as psoriasis, eczema, and vitiligo through immunomodulatory effects.

Histopathology: The microscopic examination of tissue specimens to identify cellular and structural abnormalities, providing definitive diagnosis for biopsied skin lesions.

TNM Staging: A classification system for malignant tumors based on tumor size (T), lymph node involvement (N), and distant metastasis (M), used to determine cancer stage and guide treatment decisions.

PASI Score: The Psoriasis Area and Severity Index, a quantitative scoring system that measures the severity and extent of psoriasis across body regions to assess disease burden and treatment response.

SCORAD Index: The Scoring Atopic Dermatitis index, a clinical tool that quantifies atopic dermatitis severity by combining measures of disease extent, intensity, and subjective symptoms.

DLQI: The Dermatology Life Quality Index, a patient-reported questionnaire measuring the impact of skin disease on quality of life across daily activities, relationships, and emotional well-being.

ABCDE Criteria: A clinical mnemonic for evaluating melanocytic lesions based on Asymmetry, Border irregularity, Color variation, Diameter greater than 6mm, and Evolution or change over time.

Fitzpatrick Skin Type: A classification system categorizing skin into six types (I-VI) based on response to ultraviolet light exposure, used to guide treatment parameters and assess skin cancer risk.

Dermatome: A defined area of skin innervated by a single spinal nerve, relevant to the anatomical mapping of lesions and the distribution patterns of certain dermatological conditions.

Anatomical Body Map: A standardized visual representation of body surface areas used to document lesion locations, track spatial distribution, and facilitate longitudinal comparison across visits.

Surgical Margin: The border of normal-appearing tissue surrounding an excised lesion, measured in millimeters, with width determined by diagnosis and recurrence risk.

Wound Healing Phase: A distinct stage in the tissue repair process following a dermatologic procedure, progressing through inflammatory, proliferative, and remodeling phases.

Recurrence: The reappearance of a previously treated skin condition or malignancy at or near the original site, requiring renewed clinical assessment and potentially escalated treatment.

Neuromodulator: An injectable substance such as botulinum toxin that temporarily reduces muscle activity by blocking neuromuscular signal transmission, used in cosmetic and therapeutic dermatology.

Dermal Filler: An injectable substance (hyaluronic acid, calcium hydroxylapatite, or poly-L-lactic acid) used to restore volume, smooth wrinkles, or enhance facial contours in aesthetic dermatology.

Treatment Protocol: A standardized plan specifying the type, dosage, frequency, duration, and parameters of a dermatologic treatment, based on diagnosis, severity, and patient characteristics.

Specimen Accession: The formal registration and cataloging of a biopsy specimen in the pathology laboratory, assigning a unique identifier that enables tracking through processing, analysis, and reporting.

Chain of Custody: The documented sequence of handling and transfer of a biopsy specimen from the point of collection to the pathology laboratory, ensuring specimen integrity and traceability.

Dermoscopic Pattern: A characteristic visual pattern observed during dermoscopy (such as reticular, globular, starburst, or blue-white veil) that aids in differentiating benign from malignant lesions.

Clinical Photo Documentation: The standardized capture, storage, and comparison of clinical photographs of skin conditions, used to track disease progression, treatment response, and lesion evolution over time.

Skin Biopsy Type: The specific technique used to obtain a tissue sample, including shave biopsy, punch biopsy, incisional biopsy, and excisional biopsy, each suited to different clinical scenarios.

ICD-10 Dermatology Code: A standardized diagnostic code from the International Classification of Diseases, 10th Revision, Chapter XII (L00-L99), used to classify and document skin and subcutaneous tissue disorders.

CPT Procedure Code: A Current Procedural Terminology code that identifies specific dermatologic procedures for clinical documentation, billing, and outcome tracking purposes.

Treatment Area: A precisely defined anatomical zone targeted for a specific dermatologic intervention, documented using standardized anatomical landmarks and measurement units.

Cosmetic Consent: A documented informed consent process specific to elective aesthetic procedures, covering expected outcomes, risks, alternatives, product information, and post-procedure care requirements.
