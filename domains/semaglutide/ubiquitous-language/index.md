# Semaglutide Domain - Ubiquitous Language

- **Adverse Event**: An undesirable clinical occurrence experienced by a patient during semaglutide therapy, ranging from mild gastrointestinal symptoms to serious events such as pancreatitis or medullary thyroid carcinoma.

- **BMI (Body Mass Index)**: A calculated value derived from a patient's weight and height used as a primary eligibility criterion for semaglutide therapy in the chronic weight management indication.

- **Cold Chain**: The temperature-controlled supply chain required for injectable semaglutide formulations, maintaining storage between 2 and 8 degrees Celsius from manufacturer through dispensing to preserve medication potency.

- **Contraindication**: A clinical condition or factor that makes semaglutide therapy inadvisable for a specific patient, including personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or multiple endocrine neoplasia syndrome type 2.

- **Dose Escalation**: The systematic increase of semaglutide dosage over a defined titration period, designed to improve gastrointestinal tolerability and reduce the incidence of nausea and vomiting.

- **GLP-1 Receptor Agonist**: A class of medications that mimic the incretin hormone glucagon-like peptide-1, enhancing insulin secretion, suppressing glucagon release, slowing gastric emptying, and promoting satiety.

- **HbA1c**: Glycated hemoglobin, a blood test reflecting average blood glucose levels over the preceding two to three months, used as a primary efficacy endpoint in type 2 diabetes management.

- **Indication**: An FDA-approved clinical purpose for which semaglutide may be prescribed, currently including type 2 diabetes mellitus (Ozempic, Rybelsus) and chronic weight management (Wegovy).

- **Maintenance Dose**: The target therapeutic dose of semaglutide reached after completing the titration schedule, at which the patient continues treatment for ongoing clinical benefit.

- **Off-Label Use**: The prescription of semaglutide for a clinical purpose or patient population not included in the FDA-approved labeling, requiring additional documentation and informed consent.

- **Oral Formulation (Rybelsus)**: The tablet form of semaglutide co-formulated with the absorption enhancer SNAC, taken daily on an empty stomach with no more than four ounces of water.

- **Patient Assistance Program**: A manufacturer-sponsored or third-party program that provides financial support or free medication to eligible patients who cannot afford semaglutide therapy.

- **Pharmacovigilance**: The systematic monitoring, detection, assessment, and prevention of adverse effects associated with semaglutide, including mandatory reporting to the FDA MedWatch system.

- **Prior Authorization**: An insurance requirement in which the prescriber must demonstrate medical necessity and meet payer-specific criteria before semaglutide will be covered by the patient's pharmacy benefit plan.

- **Semaglutide**: A synthetic glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor agonist with 94 percent homology to native human GLP-1, engineered for extended half-life through albumin binding and enzymatic resistance modifications.

- **Specialty Pharmacy**: A pharmacy that specializes in dispensing high-cost, complex medications such as injectable semaglutide, providing enhanced patient support services including injection training and adherence monitoring.

- **Step Therapy**: A payer-imposed protocol requiring a patient to trial and fail one or more lower-cost medications before insurance will authorize coverage of semaglutide.

- **Subcutaneous Injection**: The method of administering injectable semaglutide (Ozempic or Wegovy) via a prefilled pen into the fatty tissue of the abdomen, thigh, or upper arm, typically once weekly.

- **SUSTAIN Trials**: A series of randomized controlled clinical trials evaluating the efficacy and safety of semaglutide for type 2 diabetes, establishing its cardiovascular safety profile and glycemic control benefits.

- **STEP Trials**: A series of randomized controlled clinical trials evaluating semaglutide 2.4 mg weekly for chronic weight management, demonstrating mean body weight reductions of approximately 15 percent.

- **Titration Schedule**: A predefined protocol specifying the sequence of dose increases, time intervals between escalations, and criteria for advancing to the next dose level during semaglutide initiation.

- **Treatment Response**: The measured clinical effect of semaglutide therapy on a patient, assessed through weight loss percentage, HbA1c reduction, and patient-reported outcomes compared to baseline values.
