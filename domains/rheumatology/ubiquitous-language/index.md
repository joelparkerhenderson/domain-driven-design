# Rheumatology Domain - Ubiquitous Language

Disease Activity Score 28 (DAS28): A composite index measuring rheumatoid arthritis disease activity using 28 tender joint counts, 28 swollen joint counts, ESR or CRP, and patient global assessment.

Health Assessment Questionnaire Disability Index (HAQ-DI): A patient-reported outcome measure that evaluates functional disability across eight domains of daily living activities.

Rheumatoid Factor (RF): An autoantibody directed against the Fc portion of IgG, used as a serologic marker in the diagnosis and classification of rheumatoid arthritis.

Anti-Cyclic Citrullinated Peptide (Anti-CCP): A highly specific autoantibody for rheumatoid arthritis that can appear years before clinical disease onset and indicates more aggressive disease.

Antinuclear Antibody (ANA): An autoantibody directed against nuclear components, used as a screening test for systemic autoimmune diseases including lupus and scleroderma.

Tender Joint Count (TJC): The number of joints found to be painful upon palpation during a standardized clinical examination of specified joint groups.

Swollen Joint Count (SJC): The number of joints found to have soft tissue swelling during a standardized clinical examination, indicating active synovitis.

Treat-to-Target (T2T): A strategic treatment approach where therapy is adjusted at regular intervals until a predefined disease activity target (remission or low disease activity) is achieved.

Biologic Disease-Modifying Antirheumatic Drug (bDMARD): A class of genetically engineered proteins derived from human genes that target specific components of the immune system to reduce inflammation and disease progression.

Conventional Synthetic DMARD (csDMARD): Traditional immunomodulatory medications such as methotrexate, sulfasalazine, and hydroxychloroquine used as first-line therapy in inflammatory arthritis.

Targeted Synthetic DMARD (tsDMARD): Small molecule drugs such as JAK inhibitors that target specific intracellular signaling pathways involved in the inflammatory response.

TNF Inhibitor: A biologic agent that blocks tumor necrosis factor alpha, a key pro-inflammatory cytokine, used in the treatment of rheumatoid arthritis and other autoimmune conditions.

JAK Inhibitor: A targeted synthetic DMARD that blocks Janus kinase enzymes involved in intracellular cytokine signaling, used when conventional and biologic therapies are insufficient.

Biosimilar: A biologic product that is highly similar to an approved reference biologic with no clinically meaningful differences in safety, purity, or potency.

Infusion Protocol: A standardized procedure for intravenous administration of biologic therapies including pre-medication, infusion rate, monitoring parameters, and post-infusion observation.

Disease Flare: A clinically significant worsening of disease activity symptoms after a period of relative stability or remission, requiring treatment adjustment.

Radiographic Progression: The worsening of joint damage visible on imaging, typically measured using scoring methods such as Sharp/van der Heijde or Larsen scores.

Synovitis: Inflammation of the synovial membrane lining the joint capsule, characterized by pain, swelling, warmth, and reduced range of motion.

Crystal Arthropathy: Joint disease caused by deposition of crystals in the joint space, including monosodium urate crystals in gout and calcium pyrophosphate crystals in pseudogout.

Urate-Lowering Therapy (ULT): Medications such as allopurinol and febuxostat that reduce serum uric acid levels to prevent gout flares and dissolve existing urate crystal deposits.

SLEDAI (SLE Disease Activity Index): A validated scoring system that measures disease activity in systemic lupus erythematosus across multiple organ systems.

ACR/EULAR Classification Criteria: Standardized diagnostic criteria jointly developed by the American College of Rheumatology and European Alliance of Associations for Rheumatology for classifying rheumatic diseases.

Patient-Reported Outcome Measure (PROM): A standardized instrument completed directly by the patient to assess their perception of disease impact on health status, function, and quality of life.

Multimodal Pain Management: An integrated approach to pain control combining pharmacologic therapies, physical therapy, occupational therapy, and psychosocial interventions.

Serology Panel: A set of blood tests measuring autoantibodies and inflammatory markers used to diagnose and monitor autoimmune rheumatic diseases.

Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate (ESR): A nonspecific inflammatory marker measuring the rate at which red blood cells settle in a test tube, elevated in active inflammatory disease.

C-Reactive Protein (CRP): An acute-phase reactant protein produced by the liver in response to inflammation, used as a marker of disease activity in rheumatic conditions.

Joint Injection Therapy: The procedure of injecting corticosteroids or hyaluronic acid directly into a joint space to reduce local inflammation and pain.

OMERACT (Outcome Measures in Rheumatology): An international initiative that develops and validates outcome measures for use in rheumatology clinical trials and clinical practice.

Pharmacovigilance: The systematic monitoring, detection, assessment, and prevention of adverse effects associated with medications, particularly biologic and targeted synthetic DMARDs.
