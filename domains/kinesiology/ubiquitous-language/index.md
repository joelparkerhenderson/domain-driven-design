# Kinesiology Domain - Ubiquitous Language

## Movement Assessment Terms

- Gait Analysis: Systematic evaluation of human locomotion patterns to identify deviations from normal walking or running mechanics.
- Biomechanical Evaluation: Quantitative assessment of forces, torques, and movement patterns acting on the musculoskeletal system during functional activities.
- Range of Motion (ROM): The measurable arc of movement available at a joint, expressed in degrees and classified as active, passive, or functional.
- Functional Movement Screen (FMS): A standardized battery of seven movement tests used to identify compensatory patterns and asymmetries in fundamental movement.
- Joint Angle: The measured angle between two body segments at a specific joint, used as a primary variable in movement analysis.
- Movement Compensation: An altered movement pattern adopted by the body to accomplish a task when the ideal movement strategy is unavailable due to pain, weakness, or restriction.

## Exercise Prescription Terms

- Periodization: The systematic planning of training into distinct phases (macrocycles, mesocycles, microcycles) to optimize adaptation and minimize overtraining.
- Progressive Overload: The gradual increase of training stimulus (load, volume, intensity, or complexity) to drive continued physiological adaptation.
- Exercise Modality: A specific category of physical activity or training method, such as resistance training, cardiovascular conditioning, or flexibility work.
- Training Volume: The total amount of work performed in a training session or period, typically expressed as sets multiplied by repetitions multiplied by load.
- One-Repetition Maximum (1RM): The maximum load an individual can lift for a single repetition of a given exercise, used as a baseline for prescribing training intensity.

## Rehabilitation Terms

- Return-to-Play Criteria: A set of objective, measurable benchmarks that must be met before an athlete is cleared to resume sport participation after injury.
- Therapeutic Exercise: Structured physical activity prescribed to restore function, reduce impairment, and promote recovery following injury or surgery.
- Rehabilitation Protocol: A standardized, phase-based treatment plan that guides the progression of therapeutic interventions from initial injury through full recovery.
- Tissue Healing Phase: A distinct biological stage of tissue repair (inflammatory, proliferative, remodeling) that determines appropriate therapeutic interventions.

## Performance Analysis Terms

- Force Plate Analysis: Measurement of ground reaction forces using instrumented platforms to quantify balance, power output, and loading patterns.
- Motion Capture: Technology-assisted recording and analysis of body segment positions in three-dimensional space during movement tasks.
- Rate of Force Development (RFD): The speed at which an individual can generate muscular force, measured as the slope of the force-time curve.
- Sport-Specific Metric: A performance variable directly relevant to the demands of a particular sport, such as vertical jump height for basketball or sprint time for track.

## Injury Prevention Terms

- Screening Protocol: A systematic assessment procedure designed to identify individuals at elevated risk of injury based on movement quality, strength ratios, or training load.
- Load Management: The strategic regulation of training volume, intensity, and frequency to maintain the balance between performance adaptation and injury risk.
- Prehabilitation: Proactive exercise programming targeting known vulnerability areas to reduce future injury risk before symptoms or injury occur.
- Acute-to-Chronic Workload Ratio (ACWR): A metric comparing recent training load (acute) to longer-term average load (chronic) to monitor injury risk associated with training load changes.

## Research & Education Terms

- Evidence-Based Practice (EBP): The integration of best available research evidence with clinical expertise and patient values to inform decision-making.
- Continuing Education Unit (CEU): A standardized measure of professional development activity required to maintain professional certification and licensure.
- Clinical Practice Guideline: A systematically developed recommendation designed to assist practitioner and patient decisions about appropriate care for specific clinical circumstances.

## Cross-Cutting Terms

- Kinetic Chain: The interconnected series of body segments, joints, and muscles that work together to produce coordinated movement.
- Neuromuscular Control: The unconscious trained response of muscles to signals from the nervous system that produces coordinated, purposeful movement.
- Functional Capacity: An individual's overall ability to perform physical tasks required for daily living, occupational demands, or athletic competition.
