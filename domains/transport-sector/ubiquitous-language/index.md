# Transport Sector Domain - Ubiquitous Language

- **Bill of Lading**: A legal document issued by a carrier to a shipper that serves as a receipt for the cargo, a contract of carriage, and a document of title for the goods being transported.

- **Consignment**: A batch of goods entrusted to a carrier for transport from a consignor (sender) to a consignee (receiver), tracked as a unit throughout the freight delivery lifecycle.

- **Dead Mileage**: The distance a transport vehicle travels without carrying passengers or revenue-generating cargo, such as traveling from a depot to the start of a route, representing an operational cost to be minimized.

- **Depot**: A facility where transport vehicles are stored, maintained, fueled, and dispatched from, serving as the operational base for fleet management activities.

- **Dispatch**: The real-time assignment of a vehicle and driver to a specific route, service, or shipment, based on demand, schedule adherence, and vehicle availability.

- **Dwell Time**: The duration a vehicle remains stationary at a stop, terminal, or loading bay, encompassing passenger boarding and alighting or freight loading and unloading activities.

- **Fare**: The price charged to a passenger for a journey, calculated based on distance traveled, zones crossed, class of service, time of travel, and applicable concessions or discounts.

- **Fleet Utilization**: A metric expressing the percentage of available vehicle capacity that is actively engaged in revenue-generating service during a given period, used to assess operational efficiency.

- **Freight**: Goods or cargo transported by a carrier for commercial purposes, distinguished from passenger transport by its focus on load optimization, documentation, and delivery reliability.

- **Hours of Service (HOS)**: Regulatory limits on the maximum number of hours a commercial vehicle driver may operate within a defined period, designed to prevent fatigue-related accidents and enforce rest requirements.

- **Intermodal**: The movement of passengers or freight using two or more modes of transport (road, rail, air, maritime) within a single journey, coordinated through transfer points and standardized containers.

- **Last-Mile Delivery**: The final segment of a freight transport chain from a distribution hub to the end destination, typically the most costly and operationally challenging portion of the supply chain.

- **Load Factor**: The ratio of actual passengers or cargo carried to the total available capacity of a vehicle or service, used to evaluate service demand and revenue performance.

- **Manifest**: A detailed document listing all passengers, cargo, or freight carried on a specific vehicle trip, used for regulatory compliance, safety accountability, and operational tracking.

- **Multimodal Journey**: A passenger trip or freight movement that involves transferring between different modes of transport, requiring coordinated scheduling, ticketing, and tracking across mode boundaries.

- **Preventive Maintenance**: Scheduled maintenance activities performed on vehicles at defined intervals (time, mileage, or engine hours) to prevent breakdowns and ensure regulatory compliance with roadworthiness standards.

- **Route**: A defined path between an origin and destination with specified intermediate stops, serving as the spatial framework for scheduling, dispatching, and tracking transport services.

- **Schedule**: A planned timetable specifying the departure and arrival times for a transport service on a given route, published to inform passengers or shippers and used to measure operational punctuality.

- **Telematics**: Vehicle-mounted technology that combines GPS positioning, onboard diagnostics, and wireless communication to provide real-time data on vehicle location, speed, fuel consumption, and driver behavior.

- **Tonne-Kilometre**: A unit of freight transport output measuring one tonne of cargo transported over one kilometre, used as a standard metric for comparing freight volumes and operational productivity.

- **Waybill**: A document accompanying a freight shipment that describes the goods, their origin and destination, the route, and the freight charges, serving as an instruction to the carrier for delivery.
