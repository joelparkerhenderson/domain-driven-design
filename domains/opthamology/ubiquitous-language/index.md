# Ophthalmology Domain - Ubiquitous Language

Visual Acuity: A quantitative measure of the eye's ability to resolve detail at a standard distance, expressed as a Snellen fraction (e.g., 20/20) or LogMAR value.

Refraction: The process of determining the optical correction needed to focus light accurately on the retina, yielding a prescription for spectacles or contact lenses.

Slit Lamp Examination: A biomicroscopic examination using a focused beam of light to evaluate the anterior and posterior segments of the eye.

Tonometry: The measurement of intraocular pressure (IOP), essential for glaucoma screening and monitoring.

Intraocular Pressure (IOP): The fluid pressure inside the eye, maintained by the balance between aqueous humor production and drainage.

Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT): A non-invasive imaging modality that uses light waves to capture high-resolution cross-sectional images of retinal layers.

Fundus Photography: Photographic documentation of the interior surface of the eye, including the retina, optic disc, and macula.

Fluorescein Angiography: A diagnostic imaging procedure using intravenous fluorescein dye to visualize retinal and choroidal vasculature.

Visual Field Test: A perimetric assessment measuring the full extent of peripheral and central vision, critical for detecting glaucoma progression.

Cataract: An opacification of the crystalline lens that impairs vision, commonly treated by surgical extraction and intraocular lens implantation.

Intraocular Lens (IOL): An artificial lens implanted in the eye during cataract surgery to replace the natural crystalline lens.

Vitrectomy: A surgical procedure removing vitreous gel from the eye to treat retinal conditions such as detachment, macular holes, or vitreous hemorrhage.

Corneal Transplant: A surgical procedure replacing damaged or diseased corneal tissue with healthy donor tissue to restore vision.

Oculoplastics: A subspecialty addressing surgical and medical management of the eyelids, orbit, lacrimal system, and periocular structures.

LASIK: Laser-Assisted In Situ Keratomileusis, a refractive surgery that reshapes the cornea using an excimer laser to correct myopia, hyperopia, or astigmatism.

PRK: Photorefractive Keratectomy, a surface ablation refractive procedure that removes corneal epithelium before laser reshaping.

Glaucoma: A group of optic neuropathies characterized by progressive optic nerve damage, often associated with elevated intraocular pressure.

Diabetic Retinopathy: A microvascular complication of diabetes mellitus causing progressive damage to retinal blood vessels, potentially leading to vision loss.

Age-Related Macular Degeneration (AMD): A degenerative condition of the macula, classified as dry (atrophic) or wet (neovascular), that is the leading cause of central vision loss in older adults.

Retinal Detachment: A separation of the neurosensory retina from the underlying retinal pigment epithelium, requiring urgent surgical intervention.

Intravitreal Injection: Administration of medication directly into the vitreous cavity, commonly used for anti-VEGF therapy in retinal conditions.

Anti-VEGF Therapy: Pharmacological agents that inhibit vascular endothelial growth factor to reduce pathological neovascularization and vascular leakage.

Operative Plan: A structured clinical document specifying the surgical approach, technique, implant selection, and risk assessment for a planned ophthalmic procedure.

Imaging Study: A diagnostic investigation using one or more imaging modalities to capture structural or functional data of ocular tissues.

Treatment Plan: A documented clinical strategy specifying the therapeutic approach, medication regimen, follow-up schedule, and outcome targets for a patient condition.

Surgical Case: An aggregate representing the complete lifecycle of a surgical intervention from pre-operative assessment through post-operative recovery.

Patient Examination: An aggregate encompassing all clinical assessments performed during a single ophthalmology encounter.

Refractive Candidate Assessment: A comprehensive pre-operative evaluation determining a patient's suitability for refractive surgery based on corneal topography, pachymetry, and ocular health.

IOP Record: A timestamped measurement of intraocular pressure with associated metadata including measurement method, eye laterality, and clinical context.

Retinal Assessment: A clinical evaluation combining fundoscopic examination, imaging results, and functional testing to characterize the health and pathology of the retina.
