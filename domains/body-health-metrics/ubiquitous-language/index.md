# Body Health Metrics Domain - Ubiquitous Language

Anthropometry: The scientific measurement of body dimensions including height, weight, limb lengths, and circumferences used to assess physical characteristics.

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR): The minimum number of calories the body requires at rest to maintain essential physiological functions such as breathing and circulation.

Bioelectrical Impedance Analysis (BIA): A technique that estimates body composition by measuring the resistance of body tissues to a small electrical current.

Body Composition: The proportional breakdown of the human body into fat mass, lean mass, bone mass, and water content.

Body Fat Percentage: The proportion of total body weight that consists of adipose tissue, expressed as a percentage.

Body Mass Index (BMI): A numerical value derived from dividing weight in kilograms by the square of height in meters, used as a general indicator of body weight category.

Bone Mineral Density (BMD): A measurement of the amount of mineral content per unit area of bone, used to assess skeletal health and osteoporosis risk.

Composite Health Score: A single numerical value derived from multiple health indicators that represents overall health status.

DEXA Scan: Dual-energy X-ray absorptiometry, a clinical imaging method that precisely measures bone density, lean mass, and fat mass distribution.

Endurance Benchmark: A standardized performance threshold for sustained physical activity, such as a timed run or sustained heart rate zone.

FHIR (Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources): An international standard for exchanging electronic health records and clinical data between systems.

Flexibility Assessment: A measurement of the range of motion available at a joint or series of joints, often assessed through standardized tests such as the sit-and-reach.

Health Index: A normalized metric that combines multiple body health measurements into a single comparable value, often adjusted for age and sex.

Hydration Level: The percentage of total body weight attributable to water, reflecting fluid balance and cellular health.

Lean Body Mass: The total weight of non-fat body components including muscle, bone, organs, and connective tissue.

LOINC (Logical Observation Identifiers Names and Codes): A universal coding system for identifying medical laboratory observations and clinical measurements.

Measurement Session: A discrete event during which one or more body health metrics are recorded at a specific point in time.

Metabolic Age: An estimated biological age derived from basal metabolic rate compared to population averages for each chronological age.

Milestone: A predefined achievement threshold within a health improvement plan, such as reaching a target weight or body fat percentage.

Progress Trend: A statistical analysis of measurement changes over time, showing direction and rate of change toward a health goal.

Resting Heart Rate: The number of heartbeats per minute measured while the body is at complete rest, used as an indicator of cardiovascular fitness.

SNOMED CT: A comprehensive clinical terminology standard used to encode clinical information consistently across health information systems.

Strength Assessment: A standardized evaluation of muscular force production capacity, measured through exercises such as grip strength or one-repetition maximum.

Streak: A consecutive series of days or sessions in which a health-related activity or measurement target is maintained without interruption.

VO2 Max: The maximum rate of oxygen consumption measured during incremental exercise, considered the gold standard indicator of cardiorespiratory fitness.

Visceral Fat: Adipose tissue stored within the abdominal cavity surrounding internal organs, associated with elevated metabolic and cardiovascular risk.

Vital Signs: Fundamental clinical measurements including heart rate, blood pressure, respiratory rate, and body temperature.

Waist-to-Hip Ratio: The circumference of the waist divided by the circumference of the hips, used as an indicator of abdominal fat distribution and cardiometabolic risk.

Wellness Index: A comprehensive score that integrates physical fitness, body composition, vital signs, and lifestyle factors into a single health assessment value.

Z-Score: A statistical measure expressing how many standard deviations a given measurement falls from the population mean for a specific age and sex group.
