# Occupational Therapy Domain - Ubiquitous Language

- **Activities of Daily Living (ADL)**: Basic self-care tasks that an individual performs daily, including bathing, dressing, grooming, feeding, toileting, and functional mobility.

- **Activity Analysis**: The process of breaking down an activity into its component steps, demands, and required performance skills to identify barriers and guide therapeutic intervention.

- **Adaptive Equipment**: Specialized devices or modified tools designed to compensate for functional limitations and enable independent performance of daily activities, such as built-up utensils, button hooks, or reachers.

- **Assessment of Motor and Process Skills (AMPS)**: A standardized observational assessment that simultaneously evaluates the quality of motor and process skills during performance of familiar ADL and IADL tasks.

- **Canadian Occupational Performance Measure (COPM)**: A client-centered, semi-structured interview tool that identifies occupational performance problems, measures client self-perception of performance and satisfaction, and tracks change over time.

- **Client-Centered Practice**: An approach to OT service delivery that respects and partners with clients to prioritize their goals, values, and preferences as the foundation for assessment and intervention.

- **Cognitive Rehabilitation**: Therapeutic interventions aimed at improving or compensating for cognitive deficits in attention, memory, executive function, or visual perception that interfere with occupational performance.

- **Compensatory Strategy**: A technique or environmental modification that enables task completion by working around a functional deficit rather than remediating the underlying impairment.

- **Discharge Planning**: The collaborative process of preparing a client for transition from therapy services, including functional status documentation, home program development, equipment arrangement, and referral to community resources.

- **Executive Function**: Higher-order cognitive processes including planning, organization, sequencing, initiation, self-monitoring, and cognitive flexibility that govern purposeful, goal-directed behavior in daily activities.

- **Goal Attainment Scaling (GAS)**: A criterion-referenced measure that defines individualized, measurable therapy goals on a standardized five-point scale, enabling comparison of outcomes across clients with different conditions.

- **Instrumental Activities of Daily Living (IADL)**: Complex daily activities that require higher-level cognitive and physical skills, including meal preparation, medication management, financial management, community mobility, and household management.

- **Intervention Plan**: A documented, goal-directed treatment program specifying the therapeutic activities, intervention approaches, session frequency, duration, and measurable objectives for a client's course of occupational therapy.

- **Occupational Engagement**: Active participation in meaningful activities or occupations that provide purpose, structure, and satisfaction in a person's daily life.

- **Occupational Performance**: The ability to carry out activities of daily life, including self-care, productivity, and leisure occupations, resulting from the dynamic interaction among client factors, performance skills, and environmental contexts.

- **Occupational Profile**: A comprehensive summary of a client's occupational history, daily living patterns, interests, values, and needs that guides the evaluation process and intervention planning.

- **Occupational Therapy Practice Framework (OTPF)**: The official document of the American Occupational Therapy Association that defines the domain and process of occupational therapy practice, currently in its fourth edition (OTPF-4).

- **Performance Pattern**: An observable, recurring routine or role that shapes and is shaped by occupational engagement, including habits, routines, roles, and rituals.

- **Performance Skill**: An observable, goal-directed action that a client uses during occupational performance, categorized as motor skills, process skills, and social interaction skills.

- **Remediation**: A therapeutic approach that aims to restore or improve an impaired body function or performance skill through targeted exercise, training, or repetitive practice.

- **Splint**: A custom-fabricated or prefabricated external device applied to the hand, wrist, or upper extremity to immobilize, support, position, or correct alignment and improve functional use.

- **Therapeutic Use of Self**: The practitioner's conscious use of personality, perception, communication, and clinical reasoning to build therapeutic rapport and optimize the client's engagement in the intervention process.
