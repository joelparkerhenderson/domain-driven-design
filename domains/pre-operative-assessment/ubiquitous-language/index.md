# Pre-Operative Assessment Domain - Ubiquitous Language

- **Airway Assessment**: A structured clinical evaluation of the patient's airway anatomy and predicted difficulty of intubation, including Mallampati classification, thyromental distance, mouth opening, neck mobility, and dentition status.

- **Anaemia Optimization**: The pre-operative identification and correction of low haemoglobin levels through iron supplementation (oral or intravenous), erythropoietin therapy, or transfusion planning to reduce the need for perioperative blood transfusion.

- **Anticoagulant Bridging**: A medication management strategy in which long-term anticoagulation is temporarily replaced with a shorter-acting agent in the perioperative period to balance the risk of thrombosis against the risk of surgical bleeding.

- **ASA Physical Status Classification**: A six-category system developed by the American Society of Anesthesiologists that classifies a patient's overall health status before surgery, ranging from ASA I (healthy patient) to ASA VI (brain-dead organ donor).

- **Cardiopulmonary Exercise Testing (CPET)**: An objective, non-invasive assessment of a patient's integrated cardiovascular, pulmonary, and metabolic responses to incremental exercise, used to quantify functional capacity and predict perioperative risk.

- **Day Surgery Suitability**: The determination of whether a patient can safely undergo a surgical procedure and be discharged home on the same day, based on medical fitness, social circumstances, procedure type, and anaesthetic risk.

- **Enhanced Recovery After Surgery (ERAS)**: An evidence-based multimodal perioperative care pathway that combines pre-operative optimization, intraoperative techniques, and post-operative protocols to accelerate recovery and reduce complications.

- **Fasting Instructions**: Pre-operative guidelines specifying the duration and type of fasting required before anaesthesia, typically two hours for clear fluids and six hours for solids, to reduce the risk of pulmonary aspiration.

- **Functional Capacity**: A measure of a patient's ability to perform physical activities, estimated in metabolic equivalents (METs), used to assess cardiopulmonary reserve and predict the ability to tolerate the physiological stress of surgery and anaesthesia.

- **Glycaemic Optimization**: The pre-operative management of blood glucose levels in diabetic patients through medication adjustment, dietary guidance, and monitoring, targeting an HbA1c below 69 mmol/mol (8.5%) to reduce surgical site infection and delayed wound healing.

- **Informed Consent**: The process by which a patient, having been provided with adequate information about the proposed procedure, its risks, benefits, and alternatives, voluntarily agrees to undergo surgery.

- **Mallampati Classification**: A four-grade airway assessment scale based on the visibility of pharyngeal structures during mouth opening with tongue protrusion, used to predict the difficulty of direct laryngoscopy and endotracheal intubation.

- **Metabolic Equivalent (MET)**: A unit of functional capacity where one MET equals the oxygen consumption at rest, used to classify a patient's exercise tolerance and estimate their ability to withstand the physiological demands of surgery.

- **NICE NG45 Guidelines**: National Institute for Health and Care Excellence guidance on routine pre-operative tests for elective surgery, providing evidence-based recommendations for investigation ordering based on patient comorbidities, ASA grade, and surgical severity.

- **Perioperative Risk**: The probability of adverse outcomes (mortality, major morbidity, complications) occurring during or after surgery, estimated using patient factors, surgical factors, and validated scoring tools.

- **Pre-Assessment Clinic**: A dedicated clinical service where patients undergo structured evaluation of their fitness for surgery and anaesthesia, typically staffed by pre-assessment nurses with access to anaesthetist and physician support.

- **Pre-Operative Investigation**: A diagnostic test ordered before surgery to evaluate organ function, detect undiagnosed conditions, or establish baseline values, selected based on clinical indication rather than routine protocol.

- **Prehabilitation**: A structured program of exercise, nutrition, and psychological preparation undertaken before surgery to improve baseline functional capacity and resilience, aiming to enhance post-operative recovery.

- **Revised Cardiac Risk Index (RCRI)**: A validated six-variable clinical prediction rule that estimates the risk of major cardiac events (myocardial infarction, pulmonary oedema, cardiac arrest) after non-cardiac surgery.

- **Surgical Complexity Grading**: A classification of the magnitude of a surgical procedure (minor, intermediate, major, major-plus/complex) used to guide the level of pre-operative assessment, investigation ordering, and post-operative care planning.

- **Surgical Safety Checklist**: A standardized communication tool developed by the World Health Organization that verifies critical safety steps before induction of anaesthesia (sign in), before skin incision (time out), and before the patient leaves the operating room (sign out).

- **Triage Criteria**: A set of rules applied to surgical referrals that determine the type and urgency of pre-operative assessment required, based on patient age, comorbidities, ASA classification, and the complexity of the planned procedure.
