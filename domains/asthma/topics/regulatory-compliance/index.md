# Regulatory Compliance - Asthma Domain

## Overview

Regulatory compliance requirements shape domain model design by imposing constraints on data handling, clinical decision-making, medication management, and audit trail maintenance. In the asthma management domain, multiple regulatory frameworks apply: HIPAA governs protected health information, FDA regulations govern medication safety and clinical decision support, clinical practice guidelines establish standard of care, and quality reporting programs define measurement requirements.

Domain-Driven Design addresses regulatory compliance by embedding compliance requirements directly into domain model invariants, value object validations, and aggregate business rules. Compliance is not an afterthought or an infrastructure concern -- it is a first-class domain concept that influences how entities behave, how events are published, and how repositories persist data.

## HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act)

HIPAA governs the privacy and security of protected health information (PHI) in US healthcare systems. All six bounded contexts in the asthma domain handle PHI.

### Privacy Rule Impact on Domain Model

**Minimum Necessary Standard.** Domain events and published languages must include only the minimum PHI necessary for the consuming context to perform its function. The AssessmentFinalized event includes clinical results but not demographic details unnecessary for treatment decision-making.

**Patient Access Rights.** The domain must support patient requests to access their own data. Each bounded context must be able to produce a complete record of patient data it holds. Repository interfaces include operations that retrieve all data for a given patient.

**Accounting of Disclosures.** When PHI is shared between bounded contexts or with external systems, the disclosure must be logged. Domain events that carry PHI include disclosure tracking metadata.

**Data Retention and Destruction.** Aggregate lifecycle management must comply with state-specific medical record retention requirements (typically 6-10 years for adults, until age of majority plus retention period for minors). Repository implementations must support both retention enforcement and authorized destruction.

### Security Rule Impact on Domain Model

**Access Controls.** Domain operations enforce role-based access. A SpirometrySession can only be finalized by a qualified respiratory therapist or pulmonologist. A TreatmentPlan can only be modified by an authorized prescribing clinician. These access control rules are domain invariants, not infrastructure configuration.

**Audit Trail.** All domain operations that create, modify, or access PHI generate audit events. The domain model includes audit metadata (acting user, timestamp, action type) as part of entity state changes. StepChangeRecord entities in the Treatment Management Context serve dual purposes as both clinical documentation and audit trail.

**Data Integrity.** Finalized clinical records (assessments, spirometry sessions, treatment plans) are immutable. Corrections are made through amendment processes that preserve the original record and document the amendment rationale. This immutability is enforced by aggregate invariants.

## FDA Regulations

### Clinical Decision Support (CDS)

The FDA regulates software that provides clinical decision support under the 21st Century Cures Act. The asthma domain includes CDS functionality in several contexts:

- **AsthmaClassificationService** provides GINA severity classification -- this constitutes CDS.
- **StepwiseTherapyService** recommends treatment step changes -- this constitutes CDS.
- **BiologicEligibilityService** evaluates biologic agent eligibility -- this constitutes CDS.

CDS that meets the Cures Act exemption criteria (displays information, intended for clinical review, does not replace clinician judgment) may be exempt from device regulation. The domain model supports this by designing CDS outputs as recommendations that require clinician review and sign-off, not automated actions.

**Domain Impact:** All treatment recommendations generated by domain services are presented as suggestions requiring clinician approval. The TreatmentPlan aggregate cannot be modified without clinician authorization (sign-off entity).

### Medication Safety

FDA regulations governing medication safety directly influence the Medication Management and Treatment Management bounded contexts:

- **LABA Black Box Warning.** LABAs must not be prescribed without concurrent ICS. This FDA safety requirement is encoded as an invariant in the TreatmentPlan aggregate and validated by the PrescriptionReconciliationService.
- **Biologic Agent REMS.** Some biologic agents have Risk Evaluation and Mitigation Strategies requiring specific prescriber certifications, patient enrollment, and monitoring. The BiologicTherapy aggregate tracks REMS compliance.
- **Adverse Event Reporting.** The domain must support MedWatch reporting for adverse drug reactions. The ExacerbationEvent aggregate includes fields for suspected adverse drug reactions.

## Clinical Practice Guidelines

### Standard of Care Compliance

Clinical practice guidelines (GINA, NAEPP EPR-4) define the standard of care for asthma management. While guidelines are not regulations, deviation from established guidelines creates legal liability and quality measurement deficiencies.

**Domain Impact:** The domain model encodes guideline recommendations as configurable business rules rather than hard-coded logic. When GINA updates its stepwise therapy recommendations (as occurs annually), the TherapyStep value objects and StepwiseTherapyService can be updated without restructuring the domain model.

### Documentation Requirements

Clinical documentation standards require that certain information be recorded for legal defensibility and continuity of care:

- Assessment rationale must be documented (AssessmentNote entity).
- Treatment change rationale must be documented (StepChangeRecord entity with clinical rationale).
- Shared decision-making discussions must be documented (SharedDecisionRecord entity).
- Patient education delivery must be documented (action plan activation records).

## Quality Reporting Compliance

### HEDIS Measures

The Healthcare Effectiveness Data and Information Set (HEDIS) includes asthma-specific quality measures:

- **Medication Management for People with Asthma (MMA):** Percentage of patients who were dispensed appropriate asthma controller medications and remained on them during the treatment period. This measure drives the adherence monitoring capabilities in the Medication Management Context.
- **Asthma Medication Ratio (AMR):** Ratio of controller medications to total asthma medications. An AMR of 0.50 or higher indicates appropriate medication use. The Outcomes Tracking Context calculates this metric.

**Domain Impact:** The Outcomes Tracking Context includes quality measure calculation capabilities. The PatientOutcomeRecord aggregate tracks data elements required for HEDIS compliance reporting.

### CMS Quality Payment Program

Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services (CMS) quality measures relevant to asthma include:

- Asthma assessment documentation.
- Tobacco use screening and cessation counseling.
- Asthma action plan documentation.

**Domain Impact:** The domain model tracks action plan activation status, tobacco screening, and assessment completeness to support CMS quality measure reporting.

## Data Governance

### Data Quality Standards

- Spirometry data must meet ATS/ERS quality criteria before being used in clinical decision-making.
- Medication codes must reference authoritative drug databases (RxNorm, NDC).
- Environmental data from external sources must be validated for completeness and timeliness.
- Patient-reported outcome measures must use validated instruments (ACT, AQLQ).

### Data Interoperability Standards

- HL7 FHIR for EHR data exchange.
- NCPDP SCRIPT for electronic prescribing.
- HL7 CDA for clinical document exchange.
- ICD-10-CM for diagnosis coding (J45.x codes for asthma).
- CPT codes for procedure coding (spirometry 94010, 94060).

### Consent Management

- Patient consent for data sharing between bounded contexts must be documented.
- Research use of clinical data requires separate IRB-approved consent.
- Patient opt-out preferences must be respected across all bounded contexts.

## Compliance as Domain Invariants

Regulatory requirements are encoded as domain model invariants rather than external compliance checks:

1. **TreatmentPlan invariant:** LABA must have concurrent ICS (FDA safety rule).
2. **SpirometrySession invariant:** Quality grading required before finalization (ATS/ERS standard).
3. **PatientAssessment invariant:** Clinician sign-off required (documentation standard).
4. **Prescription invariant:** Authorized prescriber verification (state licensing requirement).
5. **BiologicTherapy invariant:** Response assessment at defined intervals (REMS compliance).
6. **AsthmaActionPlan invariant:** Emergency instructions required in red zone (standard of care).
7. **AdherenceRecord invariant:** Measurement method documented (data quality standard).
