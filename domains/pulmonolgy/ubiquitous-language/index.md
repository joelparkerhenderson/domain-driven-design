# Pulmonolgy Domain - Ubiquitous Language

Spirometry: A pulmonary function test that measures the volume and flow of air during inhalation and exhalation to assess airway obstruction and restriction.

FEV1 (Forced Expiratory Volume in 1 Second): The volume of air a patient can forcefully exhale in one second, serving as a primary metric for obstructive lung disease severity.

FVC (Forced Vital Capacity): The total volume of air a patient can forcefully exhale after maximum inhalation, used alongside FEV1 to classify ventilatory defects.

DLCO (Diffusing Capacity of the Lung for Carbon Monoxide): A measurement of the lung's ability to transfer gas from inspired air to the bloodstream, indicating alveolar-capillary membrane function.

Bronchoscopy: A diagnostic procedure using a flexible or rigid scope to visually examine the airways and obtain tissue samples for pathological analysis.

Thoracentesis: A procedure to remove fluid from the pleural space for diagnostic evaluation or therapeutic relief of pleural effusion.

Pulmonary Function Test (PFT): A comprehensive suite of breathing tests that evaluate lung volumes, capacities, flow rates, and gas exchange to characterize respiratory impairment.

Obstructive Lung Disease: A category of respiratory conditions characterized by airflow limitation due to airway narrowing, including COPD and asthma.

Restrictive Lung Disease: A category of respiratory conditions characterized by reduced lung volumes due to parenchymal, pleural, or chest wall abnormalities.

COPD (Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease): A progressive inflammatory lung disease causing irreversible airflow obstruction, encompassing emphysema and chronic bronchitis.

Asthma: A chronic inflammatory airway disease characterized by reversible bronchoconstriction, airway hyperresponsiveness, and mucus overproduction.

Interstitial Lung Disease (ILD): A group of disorders causing progressive scarring of lung tissue, impairing gas exchange and reducing lung compliance.

Pulmonary Hypertension: Elevated blood pressure in the pulmonary arteries that increases right ventricular workload and can lead to right heart failure.

Cystic Fibrosis: A genetic disorder causing thick mucus production that obstructs airways and promotes chronic respiratory infections.

ARDS (Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome): A life-threatening condition of diffuse lung inflammation and increased capillary permeability causing severe hypoxemia and bilateral pulmonary infiltrates.

Mechanical Ventilation: The use of a ventilator to deliver breaths to a patient who cannot maintain adequate gas exchange independently.

Ventilator Weaning: The systematic process of reducing ventilator support and transitioning a patient to spontaneous breathing.

Polysomnography (PSG): An overnight sleep study recording brain waves, oxygen levels, heart rate, and breathing to diagnose sleep-related breathing disorders.

Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA): A condition characterized by repetitive upper airway collapse during sleep, causing intermittent hypoxemia and sleep fragmentation.

CPAP (Continuous Positive Airway Pressure): A therapy delivering constant positive pressure through a mask to maintain airway patency during sleep.

BiPAP (Bilevel Positive Airway Pressure): A therapy delivering two levels of pressure, higher during inhalation and lower during exhalation, for patients requiring ventilatory support.

Apnea-Hypopnea Index (AHI): The number of apnea and hypopnea events per hour of sleep, used to classify OSA severity.

Exacerbation: An acute worsening of respiratory symptoms beyond normal day-to-day variation that requires a change in treatment.

Oxygen Saturation (SpO2): The percentage of hemoglobin binding sites occupied by oxygen, measured non-invasively by pulse oximetry.

Arterial Blood Gas (ABG): A blood test measuring partial pressures of oxygen and carbon dioxide, pH, and bicarbonate to assess gas exchange and acid-base status.

Peak Expiratory Flow (PEF): The maximum speed of expiration measured by a peak flow meter, used for asthma monitoring and action plan thresholds.

Pulmonary Rehabilitation: A multidisciplinary program of exercise training, breathing techniques, education, and psychosocial support to improve function in chronic lung disease patients.

Six-Minute Walk Test (6MWT): A standardized exercise test measuring the distance a patient walks in six minutes, used to assess functional capacity and treatment response.

Bronchodilator Reversibility: The improvement in airflow after administration of a bronchodilator, used to distinguish asthma from COPD and guide therapy.

Treatment Plan: A structured clinical protocol specifying medications, therapies, monitoring schedules, and self-management actions for a patient's respiratory condition.
