# Advance Decision to Refuse Treatment - Ubiquitous Language

- **Advance Decision**: A decision made by a person aged 18 or over, while they have mental capacity, to refuse specified medical treatment in the future if they later lack capacity to consent.
- **Applicability**: The determination of whether an advance decision applies to the treatment proposed in the current clinical circumstances.
- **Best Interests**: The principle that decisions made on behalf of a person who lacks capacity must be made in their best interests, considering their past and present wishes.
- **Capacity**: The ability of a person to understand, retain, weigh, and communicate information relevant to a specific decision at the time it needs to be made.
- **Capacity Assessment**: A formal evaluation conducted by a qualified professional to determine whether a person has the mental capacity to make a particular decision.
- **Clinical Applicability Check**: The process by which a healthcare professional determines whether an existing advance decision is relevant to the treatment currently being considered.
- **Donee**: A person appointed under a lasting power of attorney to make decisions on behalf of the person who created it.
- **Lasting Power of Attorney**: A legal document that allows a person to appoint someone to make decisions on their behalf, including health and welfare decisions, should they lose capacity.
- **Life-Sustaining Treatment**: Treatment that a healthcare provider considers necessary to sustain life, for which an advance decision to refuse must be in writing, signed, and witnessed.
- **Mental Capacity Act 2005**: The primary UK legislation governing decisions made on behalf of adults who lack mental capacity, including the legal framework for advance decisions.
- **Patient Self-Determination Act**: US federal legislation requiring healthcare institutions to inform patients of their right to make advance directives.
- **Revocation**: The act of withdrawing or cancelling an advance decision, which a person may do at any time while they have capacity, including orally.
- **Specified Treatment**: A particular medical treatment or class of treatments that the advance decision names as being refused.
- **Triggering Circumstances**: The specific clinical conditions or situations described in the advance decision under which the treatment refusal becomes applicable.
- **Validity**: The legal status of an advance decision, confirmed when it was made by a person with capacity, was not made under undue influence, and has not been subsequently withdrawn or overridden.
- **Witness**: A person who observes the signing of an advance decision that refuses life-sustaining treatment and adds their own signature to attest to the signing.
- **Withdrawal**: The formal or informal act of a capacitous person indicating that their advance decision is no longer to be followed.
