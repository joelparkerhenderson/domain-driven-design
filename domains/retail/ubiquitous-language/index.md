# Retail - Ubiquitous Language

Product: A specific item available for sale, identified by SKU and described by attributes such as name, category, and brand.

SKU: Stock Keeping Unit — a unique alphanumeric identifier for a specific product variant.

UPC: Universal Product Code — a barcode standard used to identify retail products at point of sale.

Category: A hierarchical classification grouping related products for browsing and merchandising.

Assortment: The curated selection of products offered at a specific store or channel.

Planogram: A visual diagram dictating product placement on shelves to optimize sales and customer experience.

Price: The monetary amount assigned to a product, which may vary by channel, location, or customer segment.

List Price: The standard retail price before any discounts or promotions are applied.

Markdown: A permanent price reduction applied to a product, typically to clear inventory.

Promotion: A temporary incentive such as a percentage discount, buy-one-get-one, or bundled offer to drive sales.

Coupon: A code or physical voucher entitling the bearer to a discount on specific products or orders.

Cart: A temporary collection of items a customer intends to purchase, whether physical or digital.

Basket: The finalized collection of items at checkout, used for transaction processing and analytics.

Transaction: A completed sale recorded at the point of sale, including items, prices, payments, and taxes.

Tender: A method of payment used to settle a transaction, such as cash, credit card, debit card, or gift card.

Receipt: A record of a completed transaction provided to the customer as proof of purchase.

Register: A physical or virtual terminal where transactions are processed.

POS: Point of Sale — the system and location where retail transactions are completed.

Barcode: A machine-readable representation of product or transaction data, typically UPC or EAN format.

Customer: A person who purchases products, identified by loyalty membership or transaction history.

Loyalty Program: A structured rewards system that incentivizes repeat purchases through points, tiers, or benefits.

Loyalty Points: A unit of reward currency earned through purchases and redeemable for discounts or products.

Member Tier: A classification level within a loyalty program based on spending or engagement thresholds.

Gift Card: A prepaid stored-value card redeemable for merchandise at participating locations.

Return: The process of a customer bringing back a purchased product for refund, exchange, or store credit.

Exchange: A return where the customer receives a different product instead of a refund.

Store Credit: A non-cash refund issued as credit toward future purchases.

Inventory: The quantity of products available for sale at a specific location or across the retail network.

Stock On Hand: The current physical count of a product at a specific location.

Reorder Point: The inventory level at which a replenishment order should be triggered.

Safety Stock: A buffer quantity maintained to prevent stockouts due to demand variability or supply delays.

Shrinkage: Inventory loss due to theft, damage, administrative error, or supplier fraud.

Receiving: The process of accepting incoming shipments from suppliers and verifying against purchase orders.

Merchandising: The practice of planning product assortments, pricing, and placement to maximize sales.

Endcap: A high-visibility display at the end of a store aisle used for promotional or featured products.

Channel: A sales pathway through which products reach customers, such as in-store, online, or mobile.

Omnichannel: An integrated approach providing seamless customer experience across all retail channels.

BOPIS: Buy Online Pick Up In Store — a fulfillment method where customers order online and collect at a physical store.

Ship From Store: A fulfillment method where online orders are shipped directly from a retail store's inventory.

Endless Aisle: A capability allowing in-store customers to order products not physically stocked at that location.

Clienteling: The practice of using customer data and preferences to provide personalized in-store service.

Supplier: An external business that provides products to the retailer through purchase orders.

Purchase Order: A formal request from the retailer to a supplier to deliver specified products at agreed terms.

Vendor Managed Inventory: An arrangement where the supplier manages inventory levels at the retailer's locations.

Department: An organizational division within a store grouping related product categories.

Season: A time-based merchandising period that drives assortment planning, pricing, and promotional strategies.

Clearance: The final markdown stage where products are sold at deeply reduced prices to eliminate remaining inventory.

Loss Prevention: The practice of reducing shrinkage through security measures, auditing, and process controls.
