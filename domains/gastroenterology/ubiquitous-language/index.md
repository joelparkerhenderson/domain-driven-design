# Gastroenterology Domain - Ubiquitous Language

## Clinical Assessment Terms

- **Clinical Encounter**: A scheduled or urgent patient visit during which a gastroenterologist evaluates symptoms, performs examination, and formulates a diagnostic or treatment plan.
- **Chief Complaint**: The primary symptom or concern reported by the patient that initiates a gastroenterology evaluation.
- **Review of Systems (ROS)**: A systematic inventory of gastrointestinal and related organ system symptoms collected during patient assessment.
- **Differential Diagnosis**: A ranked list of possible conditions that could explain a patient's presenting symptoms based on clinical evidence.
- **Alarm Feature**: A clinical finding such as unintentional weight loss, dysphagia, GI bleeding, or anemia that requires urgent diagnostic workup.

## Endoscopic Procedure Terms

- **Esophagogastroduodenoscopy (EGD)**: An upper endoscopic procedure that visualizes the esophagus, stomach, and duodenum for diagnostic or therapeutic purposes.
- **Colonoscopy**: An endoscopic examination of the entire colon and terminal ileum used for screening, surveillance, and therapeutic interventions.
- **Endoscopic Retrograde Cholangiopancreatography (ERCP)**: A specialized endoscopic procedure combining endoscopy and fluoroscopy to diagnose and treat biliary and pancreatic duct disorders.
- **Endoscopic Ultrasound (EUS)**: A procedure combining endoscopy with ultrasound imaging to evaluate lesions within or adjacent to the gastrointestinal wall.
- **Capsule Endoscopy**: A non-invasive imaging study using a swallowed wireless camera capsule to visualize the small bowel mucosa.
- **Pathology Specimen**: A tissue biopsy or fluid sample collected during an endoscopic procedure and submitted for histological analysis.

## Inflammatory Disease Terms

- **Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD)**: A chronic immune-mediated condition comprising Crohn's disease and ulcerative colitis, characterized by relapsing and remitting intestinal inflammation.
- **Crohn's Disease**: A form of IBD that can affect any segment of the gastrointestinal tract transmurally, most commonly the terminal ileum and colon.
- **Ulcerative Colitis**: A form of IBD limited to the colonic mucosa, extending proximally from the rectum in a continuous pattern.
- **Disease Flare**: An episode of increased disease activity in IBD, characterized by worsening symptoms and elevated inflammatory markers.
- **Biologic Therapy**: Targeted immunomodulatory agents such as anti-TNF, anti-integrin, or anti-IL-12/23 antibodies used to induce and maintain remission in IBD.
- **Mayo Score**: A composite clinical and endoscopic scoring system used to quantify disease activity in ulcerative colitis.

## Hepatology Terms

- **Cirrhosis**: The end-stage of chronic liver disease characterized by extensive fibrosis, nodular regeneration, and impaired hepatic function.
- **Child-Pugh Score**: A clinical scoring system using bilirubin, albumin, INR, ascites, and encephalopathy to classify the severity of cirrhosis into classes A, B, or C.
- **MELD Score**: The Model for End-Stage Liver Disease score, a numerical scale using bilirubin, creatinine, and INR to predict short-term mortality and prioritize organ allocation for liver transplantation.
- **Hepatitis**: Inflammation of the liver caused by viral infection (hepatitis A, B, C, D, E), autoimmune processes, metabolic conditions, or toxic exposures.
- **Hepatic Encephalopathy**: A neuropsychiatric syndrome resulting from impaired hepatic detoxification, presenting as confusion, asterixis, and altered consciousness.

## Motility Disorder Terms

- **Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD)**: A chronic condition in which gastric acid refluxes into the esophagus, causing symptoms such as heartburn and regurgitation, potentially leading to esophageal mucosal injury.
- **Esophageal Manometry**: A diagnostic study that measures pressure and coordination of esophageal muscle contractions to evaluate swallowing disorders and esophageal motility.
- **pH Monitoring**: An ambulatory diagnostic study that measures esophageal acid exposure over 24 to 96 hours to quantify gastroesophageal reflux.
- **Rome IV Criteria**: The internationally recognized diagnostic framework for classifying functional gastrointestinal disorders based on symptom patterns rather than structural findings.
- **Functional Dyspepsia**: A Rome IV-defined disorder characterized by postprandial fullness, early satiation, or epigastric pain without identifiable structural cause.

## Nutrition Management Terms

- **Enteral Nutrition**: Nutritional support delivered directly to the gastrointestinal tract via oral supplements or tube feeding for patients unable to meet nutritional needs through normal diet.
- **Parenteral Nutrition**: Intravenous delivery of nutrients including amino acids, lipids, glucose, vitamins, and minerals for patients with non-functional or inaccessible gastrointestinal tracts.
- **Celiac Disease**: An autoimmune enteropathy triggered by gluten ingestion in genetically susceptible individuals, causing villous atrophy and malabsorption.
- **Food Intolerance**: A non-immune-mediated adverse reaction to specific foods or food components, such as lactose intolerance or fructose malabsorption, causing gastrointestinal symptoms.
- **Nutritional Assessment**: A comprehensive evaluation of a patient's dietary intake, anthropometric measurements, biochemical markers, and clinical signs to determine nutritional status and guide intervention.
