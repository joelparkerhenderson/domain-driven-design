# Fulfillment - Ubiquitous Language

Fulfillment Order: An internal representation of a customer order within the fulfillment context, containing items to pick, pack, and ship.

Order Line: A single item entry within a fulfillment order, specifying SKU, quantity, and allocation status.

SKU: Stock Keeping Unit — a unique identifier for a specific product variant in inventory.

Pick List: A document or digital instruction set directing warehouse workers to collect items from storage locations.

Pick: The act of retrieving an item from its warehouse location to fulfill an order.

Pack: The process of placing picked items into shipping containers with appropriate packaging materials.

Shipment: A collection of packages dispatched together to a single destination address.

Package: A physical container holding one or more items prepared for shipping.

Carrier: A logistics provider responsible for transporting packages from warehouse to destination.

Tracking Number: A unique identifier assigned by a carrier to track a package through the delivery network.

Shipping Label: A printed or digital label containing carrier routing information, barcodes, and destination address.

Manifest: A summary document listing all packages and their contents for a given shipment or carrier pickup.

Warehouse: A physical facility where inventory is stored, picked, packed, and shipped.

Zone: A designated area within a warehouse organized by product type, temperature, or picking efficiency.

Bin: A specific storage location within a warehouse zone identified by a unique location code.

Inventory: The total stock of goods available for fulfillment at a given warehouse or across the network.

Allocation: The process of reserving specific inventory units to fulfill a particular order.

Reservation: A temporary hold on inventory units pending order confirmation or fulfillment.

Backorder: An order or order line that cannot be fulfilled immediately due to insufficient inventory.

Split Shipment: An order fulfilled in multiple shipments when items are in different locations or available at different times.

Wave: A batch of orders grouped for simultaneous picking to optimize warehouse efficiency.

Batch Pick: Picking items for multiple orders simultaneously to reduce travel time in the warehouse.

Put-Away: The process of receiving inbound goods and placing them in designated warehouse locations.

Receiving: The process of accepting, inspecting, and recording incoming inventory shipments at a warehouse.

Last Mile: The final delivery leg from the last distribution hub to the customer's address.

Proof of Delivery: Documentation confirming successful delivery, including signature, photo, or timestamp.

Return Merchandise Authorization: A formal approval for a customer to return a product, assigned a unique RMA number.

Return: The process of a customer sending back a received product for refund, exchange, or repair.

Exception: An unexpected event during fulfillment requiring special handling, such as damaged goods or address errors.

Service Level Agreement: A commitment defining expected fulfillment speed, such as same-day, next-day, or standard shipping.

Cut-Off Time: The daily deadline by which orders must be received to qualify for same-day or next-day processing.

Weight: The measured mass of a package, used for carrier rate calculation and shipping label generation.

Dimensions: The length, width, and height of a package, used for carrier rate calculation and space planning.

Address: A structured representation of a delivery destination including street, city, state, postal code, and country.

Rate: The cost charged by a carrier for shipping a package based on weight, dimensions, destination, and service level.

Duty: Import tax or tariff applied to international shipments based on goods classification and destination country.

Customs Declaration: Documentation describing package contents and value for international border clearance.
