# Genetics Domain - Ubiquitous Language

- **ACMG/AMP Classification**: A five-tier variant classification system (pathogenic, likely pathogenic, variant of uncertain significance, likely benign, benign) established by the American College of Medical Genetics and Genomics and the Association for Molecular Pathology.

- **Allele**: One of two or more alternative forms of a gene or genetic locus that occupy the same position on a chromosome.

- **Autosomal Dominant Inheritance**: A pattern of heredity in which a single copy of a disease-causing variant on a non-sex chromosome is sufficient to cause the associated condition.

- **Autosomal Recessive Inheritance**: A pattern of heredity in which two copies of a disease-causing variant on a non-sex chromosome are required for the associated condition to manifest.

- **Carrier**: An individual who possesses one copy of a recessive pathogenic variant without manifesting clinical symptoms but who can pass the variant to offspring.

- **Carrier Screening**: A genetic test performed to determine whether an individual carries a pathogenic variant for a recessive or X-linked condition, typically offered in the preconception or prenatal setting.

- **Cascade Testing**: The systematic process of testing at-risk biological relatives after a pathogenic variant has been identified in a proband within a family.

- **ClinVar**: A public archive maintained by the National Center for Biotechnology Information (NCBI) that aggregates information about the relationships between human genetic variants and observed health conditions.

- **Copy Number Variant (CNV)**: A structural genomic alteration in which sections of DNA are duplicated or deleted, resulting in a deviation from the expected diploid number of copies.

- **Exome Sequencing**: A genomic technique that selectively sequences all protein-coding regions (exons) of the genome, representing approximately 1-2% of total genomic DNA.

- **Genetic Counseling**: A communication process conducted by trained professionals that helps individuals and families understand and adapt to the medical, psychological, and familial implications of genetic contributions to disease.

- **Genome Sequencing**: The comprehensive determination of the complete DNA sequence of an organism's genome, including both coding and non-coding regions.

- **Genotype**: The specific combination of alleles present at a particular genetic locus or set of loci in an individual.

- **HGVS Nomenclature**: The standardized system established by the Human Genome Variation Society for describing sequence variants in DNA, RNA, and protein, ensuring unambiguous communication of variant identity.

- **Mendelian Disorder**: A genetic condition caused by variants in a single gene that follows predictable inheritance patterns described by Gregor Mendel.

- **Metabolizer Phenotype**: A pharmacogenomic classification (poor, intermediate, normal, rapid, or ultrarapid) that predicts how efficiently an individual metabolizes specific drugs based on their genotype.

- **Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS)**: High-throughput DNA sequencing technologies that enable massively parallel sequencing of millions of fragments simultaneously.

- **Non-Invasive Prenatal Testing (NIPT)**: A screening method that analyzes cell-free fetal DNA circulating in maternal blood to assess the risk of chromosomal aneuploidies and other genetic conditions.

- **Pathogenicity**: The capacity of a genetic variant to cause or contribute to disease, assessed through population frequency data, computational predictions, functional studies, and segregation evidence.

- **Pedigree**: A standardized diagram depicting the biological relationships and medical history within a family, used to analyze inheritance patterns and calculate disease risk.

- **Penetrance**: The proportion of individuals carrying a particular pathogenic variant who actually manifest the associated clinical phenotype.

- **Pharmacogenomics**: The study of how genetic variation influences an individual's response to medications, including drug efficacy, dosing requirements, and adverse reaction risk.

- **Phenotype**: The observable physical, biochemical, or clinical characteristics of an individual resulting from the interaction of genotype and environment.

- **Preimplantation Genetic Testing (PGT)**: The genetic analysis of embryos created through in vitro fertilization prior to uterine transfer, used to detect monogenic disorders, chromosomal abnormalities, or structural rearrangements.

- **Variant of Uncertain Significance (VUS)**: A genetic variant for which the available evidence is insufficient or conflicting to determine whether it is pathogenic or benign.
