# Autism Domain: Ubiquitous Language

ABA (Applied Behavior Analysis): Evidence-based therapeutic approach that applies principles of learning and behavior to improve socially significant behaviors in individuals with autism.

ADOS-2 (Autism Diagnostic Observation Schedule, Second Edition): Standardized semi-structured assessment of communication, social interaction, and play used as the gold standard diagnostic instrument for autism.

AAC (Augmentative and Alternative Communication): Communication methods and devices used to supplement or replace spoken language for individuals with communication challenges.

Antecedent: An event or condition that occurs immediately before a target behavior and may trigger or set the occasion for that behavior.

Behavior Intervention Plan (BIP): A formal document describing strategies to address challenging behaviors by modifying antecedents, teaching replacement behaviors, and adjusting consequences.

BCBA (Board Certified Behavior Analyst): A graduate-level professional certified by the BACB to design, implement, and supervise applied behavior analysis programs.

Caregiver: A parent, guardian, or family member who provides primary support and advocacy for an individual with autism across home and community settings.

Consequence: An event or condition that follows a target behavior and influences the future probability of that behavior occurring.

Developmental Milestone: A specific skill or behavior expected to emerge at a particular age, used to track an individual's developmental progress over time.

Discrete Trial Training (DTT): A structured ABA teaching method that breaks skills into small components taught through repeated trials with clear antecedents, prompts, and reinforcement.

DSM-5 Diagnostic Criteria: The diagnostic standards published in the Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders, Fifth Edition, used to formally diagnose autism spectrum disorder.

Echolalia: The repetition of words or phrases spoken by others, which may serve communicative or self-regulatory functions for individuals with autism.

Environmental Modification: A change made to a physical space or setting to reduce sensory overload, support regulation, or promote functional engagement.

Functional Behavior Assessment (FBA): A systematic process for identifying the function or purpose of a challenging behavior to inform intervention planning.

IEP (Individualized Education Program): A legally mandated document that outlines educational goals, services, accommodations, and supports for a student with a disability in a public school.

Inclusive Education: An educational approach that integrates students with autism into general education classrooms with appropriate supports and accommodations.

M-CHAT-R/F (Modified Checklist for Autism in Toddlers, Revised with Follow-Up): A validated screening tool used to identify toddlers at risk for autism during routine well-child visits.

Masking: The conscious or unconscious suppression of autistic traits by an individual to conform to social expectations, which can lead to burnout and delayed diagnosis.

Natural Environment Teaching (NET): An ABA approach that uses naturally occurring opportunities, interests, and motivations to teach skills in everyday settings.

PECS (Picture Exchange Communication System): A structured AAC intervention that teaches individuals to communicate by exchanging picture symbols with a communication partner.

Prompt: A cue or assistance provided to help an individual perform a target behavior, which is systematically faded as the individual gains independence.

Reinforcement: A consequence that increases the future probability of a behavior, which may be positive (adding a preferred stimulus) or negative (removing an aversive stimulus).

Sensory Diet: A personalized plan of sensory activities scheduled throughout the day to help an individual maintain optimal arousal and self-regulation.

Sensory Profile: A comprehensive assessment of an individual's sensory processing patterns across visual, auditory, tactile, vestibular, proprioceptive, olfactory, and gustatory systems.

Sensory Threshold: The neurological boundary at which a sensory stimulus is detected or becomes overwhelming, varying across sensory modalities and individuals.

Skill Acquisition Program: A structured plan within ABA therapy that targets the development of a specific skill through systematic instruction and data collection.

Social Communication: The use of language and nonverbal cues in social contexts, including joint attention, conversational reciprocity, and pragmatic language skills.

Stimming (Self-Stimulatory Behavior): Repetitive movements, sounds, or actions that serve self-regulatory, communicative, or enjoyment functions for individuals with autism.

Transition Planning: A coordinated set of activities beginning at age 16 that prepares a student with autism for post-secondary education, employment, and independent living.

Treatment Effectiveness: A measure of how well an intervention achieves its intended outcomes, evaluated through systematic data collection and progress monitoring.
