# Ubiquitous Language: Autism Domain

The ubiquitous language establishes shared terminology used consistently by domain experts, developers, and stakeholders across the autism spectrum management domain.

## Purpose

The autism domain involves professionals from multiple disciplines -- diagnosticians, behavior analysts, occupational therapists, speech-language pathologists, and special educators -- each with their own professional vocabulary. The ubiquitous language bridges these disciplines by defining terms that carry precise, agreed-upon meanings within each bounded context and across the domain as a whole. This shared vocabulary eliminates ambiguity in requirements, design discussions, and system implementation.

## Language Development Process

The ubiquitous language for the autism domain is developed through collaborative workshops with domain experts from each bounded context. Terms are defined, refined, and validated against clinical guidelines (DSM-5, BACB ethics code, ASHA practice guidelines) and educational regulations (IDEA, ADA). When the same word carries different meanings in different professional contexts, the ubiquitous language specifies context-specific definitions to prevent confusion.

## Context-Specific Terminology

### Diagnostic Assessment Context

Terms in this context align with clinical diagnostic vocabulary. "Assessment" refers specifically to a formal evaluation using standardized instruments. "Screening" refers to a preliminary check that identifies individuals who may benefit from a full assessment. "Diagnosis" refers to the formal determination that an individual meets DSM-5 criteria for autism spectrum disorder. "Severity level" maps to the DSM-5 three-tier support needs classification.

### Behavioral Intervention Context

Terms in this context align with ABA professional vocabulary. "Behavior" refers to an observable and measurable action. "Intervention" refers to a systematic procedure designed to change behavior. "Reinforcement" and "punishment" carry their precise operant conditioning meanings, not colloquial interpretations. "Mastery" means meeting predetermined criteria for skill acquisition.

### Sensory Processing Context

Terms in this context align with occupational therapy vocabulary. "Sensory modulation" refers to the ability to regulate responses to sensory input. "Threshold" refers to the neurological point at which sensory input is detected or becomes overwhelming. "Sensory diet" is a prescribed plan of sensory activities, not a food-related concept.

### Communication Support Context

Terms in this context align with speech-language pathology vocabulary. "Communication" encompasses both verbal and nonverbal expression. "Augmentative" refers to methods that supplement existing speech. "Alternative" refers to methods that replace speech entirely. "Pragmatic language" refers to the social use of language in context.

### Educational Planning Context

Terms in this context align with special education regulatory language. "Accommodation" refers to changes in how instruction is delivered without altering content standards. "Modification" refers to changes in what a student is expected to learn. "Least restrictive environment" is a legal requirement, not a suggestion.

### Outcomes Tracking Context

Terms in this context align with measurement and evaluation vocabulary. "Outcome" refers to a measurable result of an intervention. "Effectiveness" refers to the degree to which an intervention achieves its intended outcome. "Progress" refers to change measured over time against established baselines.

## Cross-Context Term Disambiguation

Some terms require explicit disambiguation because they carry different meanings across contexts:

- "Goal": In the Behavioral Intervention Context, a goal is a measurable behavioral target. In the Educational Planning Context, a goal is an IEP objective with specific criteria and timelines. In the Communication Support Context, a goal is a speech-language therapy target.

- "Assessment": In the Diagnostic Assessment Context, it is a formal diagnostic evaluation. In the Sensory Processing Context, it is a sensory profile evaluation. In the Educational Planning Context, it is an evaluation to determine special education eligibility.

- "Plan": In the Behavioral Intervention Context, a plan is a behavior intervention plan (BIP). In the Sensory Processing Context, a plan is a sensory diet. In the Educational Planning Context, a plan is an IEP or transition plan.

## Language Governance

The ubiquitous language is maintained through regular review sessions involving domain experts from all six bounded contexts. Any proposed changes to shared terminology must be reviewed for impact across all contexts before adoption.
