# Subdomains: Autism Domain

Subdomains classify the areas of the autism spectrum management domain by their strategic importance, guiding investment decisions and implementation approaches.

## Purpose

Not all parts of a domain system carry equal strategic value. Subdomain classification helps organizations allocate resources effectively by distinguishing between areas that provide competitive advantage (core), areas that support the core but follow established patterns (supporting), and areas that can leverage commodity solutions (generic). In the autism domain, this classification reflects the relative complexity and differentiation potential of each area.

## Core Subdomains

Core subdomains represent the highest-value, most complex areas that define the unique capabilities of the autism management system.

### Diagnostic Assessment

The diagnostic assessment subdomain is core because accurate, timely diagnosis is the foundation of all subsequent interventions. The complexity of integrating multiple standardized instruments (M-CHAT-R/F, ADOS-2, ADI-R), applying DSM-5 criteria, coordinating multi-disciplinary team evaluations, and managing diagnostic waitlists requires deep domain expertise. Diagnostic accuracy directly determines the quality of all downstream care. No off-the-shelf solution adequately captures the nuanced clinical reasoning involved in autism diagnosis.

### Behavioral Intervention

The behavioral intervention subdomain is core because ABA therapy is the most extensively researched and widely implemented evidence-based intervention for autism. The complexity of designing individualized behavior intervention plans, managing skill acquisition programs across multiple developmental domains, collecting and analyzing session-level data, and adapting interventions based on ongoing assessment requires sophisticated domain modeling. This subdomain demands the highest investment in domain expert collaboration and model refinement.

## Supporting Subdomains

Supporting subdomains provide essential specialized capabilities that enhance the core but follow more established professional patterns.

### Sensory Processing

The sensory processing subdomain supports the core by addressing sensory processing differences that significantly impact behavior and communication. While sensory assessment and intervention follow established occupational therapy frameworks (Sensory Profile 2, Ayres Sensory Integration), the integration of sensory data with behavioral and communication interventions requires careful modeling. Sensory profiles directly inform behavior intervention plans and environmental accommodations.

### Communication Support

The communication support subdomain supports the core by addressing the communication challenges central to autism. Speech-language pathology and AAC interventions follow established professional protocols (PECS six-phase protocol, AAC feature matching), but the integration of communication data with behavioral outcomes and educational goals requires domain-specific modeling. Functional communication training bridges the behavioral and communication subdomains.

## Generic Subdomains

Generic subdomains follow well-established patterns that can leverage existing frameworks and industry solutions.

### Educational Planning

The educational planning subdomain is generic because IEP development, transition planning, and special education service delivery follow legally mandated processes (IDEA, ADA) that are consistent across disability categories. While autism-specific accommodations and goals are developed within this subdomain, the overall IEP lifecycle, compliance requirements, and documentation standards are shared across special education generally. Existing special education management systems can be adapted for autism-specific needs.

### Outcomes Tracking

The outcomes tracking subdomain is generic because progress monitoring, outcome measurement, and longitudinal analysis follow established healthcare analytics and educational measurement patterns. Standard data aggregation, reporting, and visualization frameworks can be configured for autism-specific metrics. The primary value of this subdomain lies in integration with core and supporting subdomains rather than in novel modeling.

## Subdomain Interactions

Core subdomains drive the requirements for supporting and generic subdomains. Diagnostic assessment findings flow into behavioral intervention design. Behavioral data informs sensory and communication adjustments. All intervention data feeds into outcomes tracking, which in turn informs diagnostic reassessment and intervention modification.

## Investment Implications

- Core subdomains warrant the most investment in domain expert collaboration, model refinement, and custom development.
- Supporting subdomains benefit from established professional frameworks but require domain-specific adaptation and integration work.
- Generic subdomains can leverage existing platforms and standards with configuration and integration effort.

## Reclassification Considerations

Subdomain classifications should be reviewed periodically. For example, if an organization develops proprietary sensory-behavioral integration algorithms, the Sensory Processing subdomain may be reclassified as core. Conversely, as autism-specific ABA platforms mature, some aspects of the Behavioral Intervention subdomain may become generic.
