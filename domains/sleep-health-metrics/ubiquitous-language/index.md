# Sleep Health Metrics - Ubiquitous Language

Polysomnography (PSG): A comprehensive multi-channel sleep study recording brain waves, eye movements, muscle activity, heart rhythm, and respiratory effort during sleep.

Actigraphy: A non-invasive method of monitoring rest-activity cycles using a wrist-worn accelerometer to estimate sleep-wake patterns over extended periods.

Sleep Epoch: A 30-second segment of polysomnographic data that serves as the fundamental unit for sleep stage scoring according to AASM criteria.

Sleep Stage: A classification of a sleep epoch into one of the recognized states: Wake (W), Non-REM Stage 1 (N1), Non-REM Stage 2 (N2), Non-REM Stage 3 (N3), or REM (R).

Sleep Architecture: The structural organization of sleep across the night, described by the proportion, duration, and sequencing of sleep stages.

Sleep Latency: The duration of time measured from lights-out to the onset of the first epoch scored as sleep.

REM Latency: The duration of time from sleep onset to the first epoch of REM sleep.

Wake After Sleep Onset (WASO): The total amount of wake time occurring after initial sleep onset and before final awakening.

Sleep Efficiency: The ratio of total sleep time to total time in bed, expressed as a percentage.

Total Sleep Time (TST): The cumulative duration of all epochs scored as sleep within the recording period.

Apnea-Hypopnea Index (AHI): The number of apnea and hypopnea events per hour of sleep, used to classify obstructive sleep apnea severity.

Arousal Index: The number of cortical arousals per hour of sleep, reflecting sleep fragmentation.

Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index (PSQI): A validated self-report questionnaire measuring subjective sleep quality and disturbances over a one-month period, yielding a global score from 0 to 21.

Epworth Sleepiness Scale (ESS): A self-administered questionnaire that measures daytime sleepiness propensity across eight common situations, scored from 0 to 24.

Circadian Rhythm: The approximately 24-hour internal biological clock that regulates the sleep-wake cycle, body temperature, hormone secretion, and other physiological processes.

Chronotype: An individual's intrinsic preference for the timing of sleep and wakefulness, ranging from extreme morning type (early chronotype) to extreme evening type (late chronotype).

Melatonin: A hormone secreted by the pineal gland in response to darkness that signals the biological night and promotes sleep onset.

Dim Light Melatonin Onset (DLMO): The time at which melatonin concentration rises above a threshold under dim light conditions, serving as the most reliable marker of circadian phase.

Sleep Hygiene: A set of behavioral and environmental recommendations designed to promote healthy sleep, including consistent sleep schedules, dark quiet environments, and avoidance of stimulants before bedtime.

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia (CBT-I): An evidence-based structured therapeutic program that addresses dysfunctional thoughts and behaviors contributing to chronic insomnia.

Continuous Positive Airway Pressure (CPAP): A device therapy that delivers pressurized air through a mask to maintain upper airway patency during sleep in patients with obstructive sleep apnea.

CPAP Adherence: The measure of patient compliance with prescribed CPAP therapy, typically defined as usage of four or more hours per night on at least 70 percent of nights.

Sleep Diary: A daily self-report log in which patients record bedtime, wake time, sleep latency, nighttime awakenings, and subjective sleep quality.

Hypnogram: A graphical representation of sleep architecture over time, plotting sleep stages on the vertical axis against elapsed time on the horizontal axis.

AASM Scoring Manual: The American Academy of Sleep Medicine manual that provides standardized rules and criteria for scoring sleep stages and associated events.

ICSD-3: The International Classification of Sleep Disorders, 3rd Edition, providing the diagnostic taxonomy for sleep medicine.

FHIR (Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources): An HL7 standard for exchanging healthcare information electronically, used for clinical integration of sleep health data.

Sleep Fragmentation: The disruption of sleep continuity by arousals or awakenings, leading to reduced sleep quality independent of total sleep time.

Slow Wave Sleep (SWS): The deepest stage of non-REM sleep (N3), characterized by high-amplitude delta waves, associated with physical restoration and memory consolidation.

REM Sleep Behavior Disorder (RBD): A parasomnia characterized by loss of normal skeletal muscle atonia during REM sleep, resulting in dream enactment behaviors.

Sleep-Disordered Breathing (SDB): A spectrum of respiratory abnormalities during sleep, ranging from primary snoring to severe obstructive sleep apnea syndrome.
